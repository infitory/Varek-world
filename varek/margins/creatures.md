# Creatures of Varek

> **Type:** Bestiary (the non-ordinary inhabitants)
> **Status:** Canonical

[Varek](../world/overview.md) has always had, in the edges of its forests and beneath its older stones, creatures and phenomena that do not fit the ordinary categories of animal or man. Most of them have been there since before recorded history. They are uncommon. They are not, in themselves, an apocalypse.

They are part of the texture of the world — the way the weather is part of the texture. You respect them. You avoid them. You do not, generally, die of them, unless you are foolish.

**What is different now** is not that these things exist. It is that there are **more of them**, in **more places**, and some of them are **different from what the old accounts describe**.

---

## Hollow-men

> **Type:** Humanoid, old
> **Habitat:** Deep forests, old ruins, abandoned sites
> **Aggression:** Low; defensive
> **Rarity:** Uncommon but stable

Humanoid creatures found in the deep forests and certain old ruins. Look roughly like men. Move roughly like men. Are not men.

### Description

Hollow-men are tall — usually slightly taller than the Varekian average. Thin. Grey-skinned or bark-coloured, depending on habitat. Their faces are recognisably human in shape but **lack certain details** — expressions are limited, eyes are uniformly dark without visible iris, the mouth does not move when they breathe. They wear, often, the remnants of human clothing that has become part of them — fabric grown into skin, a belt fused to a hip.

Hollow-men do not speak. They are said to occasionally **hum** — a low, tuneless sound, usually at dusk.

### Behaviour

Hollow-men are slow, largely non-aggressive unless cornered, and subsist on things that men do not eat: certain fungi, certain barks, certain stones broken and ground to powder. They do not hunt.

A hollow-man will usually avoid a living human. If a human approaches one, it will retreat. If a human corners one, it will become motionless and wait. If a human attacks one, it will defend itself — but even in defence, its movements are slow and seem reluctant.

Hollow-men have been observed tending to each other — grooming, sharing food, sitting together for long periods in silence. They may be social in ways humans do not recognise.

### Origin

Contested. The prevailing scholarly view is that hollow-men are **descendants of humans** who became something else over many generations, possibly as a result of prolonged exposure to [Old World](../history/old-world.md) residue. They are not undead. They are not inhuman. They are something that began as human and has moved, slowly, elsewhere.

A minority scholarly view holds that hollow-men are humans who **survived the First Collapse** in ways the Old World's magic preserved — and that what survives is not the person but a kind of residue that persists indefinitely.

The Old Rites practitioners have their own view: hollow-men are *what the dead become if they are not properly buried with the correct words.* The view is not widely believed by scholars but is widely believed by those who live near hollow-men.

### Recent changes

**None that have been confirmed.** Hollow-men appear to be behaving now as they have for centuries.

However: hollow-men have been seen in places where they have not historically been seen. Not in large numbers. But they have appeared in parts of the Ashfields and the southern Greyspine where no reliable records indicate them before. Whether they are migrating, or whether they have always been there and are simply becoming more visible, is not clear.

## Night-calves

> **Type:** Mimic creatures
> **Habitat:** Hollow Lands, parts of the Vorrwood, certain rural fields
> **Aggression:** None, if ignored
> **Rarity:** Rare

A category of creatures that resemble livestock but are not. Found in the [Hollow Lands](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-hollow-lands) and parts of the [Vorrwood](../world/regions.md#the-vorrlands). Approach farmsteads at night. Appear to low and graze.

### Description

A night-calf looks, at a casual glance, like a calf. It is roughly the right size, roughly the right shape, and behaves as a calf would — it grazes, it lows, it wanders slowly.

Look closely and the details fail. The eyes are too large. The hooves are too many, or the wrong number. The legs bend in directions no calf's should. The hair is too uniform, or patterned in ways that resemble writing. The sounds it makes, heard attentively, are not quite the sounds a calf makes — they are *almost* those sounds, as if recreated by something that has studied calves carefully but has never been one.

### Behaviour

Night-calves are not aggressive. They do not attack. They simply *appear* — wandering onto farmland at night, mingling with actual livestock, standing in dooryards.

**If they are engaged with — milked, slaughtered, fed — something bad happens.** The specifics vary by account:

- A farmer who slaughtered a night-calf for meat reported that the meat, when cooked, made sounds. His family became ill. Two died.
- A woman who attempted to milk a night-calf said the milk came out dark. She poured it away. The patch of ground where it fell produced nothing for three years afterward.
- A farmhand who fed a night-calf from his hand found that his hand, over the following weeks, became numb, then waxy, then had to be amputated.

The correct response to a night-calf is to **ignore it completely**. Do not approach. Do not feed. Do not touch. Do not drive it away. Simply continue as if it is not there. By morning, it usually is not.

### Why they come

Unknown. The night-calf phenomenon is widely attested but poorly understood. Theories range from "they are testing for something" to "they are emissaries of [the Hollow Lands'](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-hollow-lands) ecology" to "they are asking for something and hoping a human will guess correctly."

### Recent changes

Night-calves have been seen **further from their traditional ranges** in the last two years. Reports have emerged from villages in the eastern Ashfields that had no prior history of them. The night-calves behave the same as they always have. They are simply appearing in new places.

## Reader-birds

> **Type:** Small birds (possibly a natural species, possibly not)
> **Habitat:** Anywhere, but only briefly
> **Aggression:** None
> **Rarity:** Uncommon; increasingly common

Small black birds that appear in the hours before a death in certain households. Widely considered a courtesy rather than a threat.

### Description

A reader-bird looks like a small, ordinary black bird — perhaps a sparrow or a wren in silhouette. But they are recognisable by behaviour: a reader-bird enters a house (through an open window, a chimney, occasionally a gap in the thatch), sits quietly in a visible spot, and **watches**.

It does not fly around the house. It does not eat food set out for it. It does not sing. It sits, usually for several hours, and then leaves.

Someone in the household dies soon after. Usually within the day. Occasionally within the hour.

### Behaviour

Reader-birds do not cause the death. They precede it. The prevailing understanding is that they are **paying attention** to something that is already happening — a death that is already in motion, which they can somehow perceive.

The tradition in most Varekian regions is to **not disturb a reader-bird**. Do not chase it away. Do not try to kill it. Do not take it as an omen and panic. Simply continue. If a reader-bird has come, the death is already coming; the bird is not bringing it.

Some households treat the arrival of a reader-bird as a sign to **prepare** — to call the [Quiet](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-quiet) clergy, to gather the family, to open the dying one's papers. This is considered wise.

### Origin

Disputed. They may be a natural species with an unusual ability. They may be something else wearing the shape of a bird. They may be associated with one of the [Undergods](../faiths/buried-faiths.md#the-undergods) or with the [Quiet](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-quiet) itself — though the Quiet clergy officially deny any connection.

### Recent changes

**Increasingly common in the last three years.** Sightings are up across Varek. Reader-birds are appearing in households where deaths follow — but also, in a smaller number of cases, in households where **deaths do not follow, or do not follow immediately**.

This is new. The tradition holds that reader-birds are infallible. The recent cases of "false" readings suggest either that the tradition was wrong, or that something has changed.

Some [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md) practitioners believe the reader-birds are now responding to **deaths that have not yet happened but are going to happen**, on longer time-scales. This is consistent with their increased activity and their occasional "false" readings.

Others believe the reader-birds are now paying attention to **something other than death**. What that something is, they do not know.

## Stone-sleepers

> **Type:** Unknown; large; possibly mineral
> **Habitat:** Within certain large rocks in the Greyspine
> **Aggression:** Unknown; no direct contact has ever been confirmed
> **Rarity:** Specific sites; very rare

Beings, perhaps beings, that live **within** certain large rocks in the [Greyspine](../world/regions.md#the-greyspine). Never seen. Occasionally heard — a slow, deep sound that miners describe as **breathing**.

### Evidence

Stone-sleeper presence is indicated by:

- **The breathing sound** — a slow, rhythmic, very low-frequency sound that seems to come from inside large rocks. Heard through solid stone. Not heard by everyone; some people hear it clearly, others not at all.
- **Warmth** — a stone-sleeper rock is warmer than the surrounding stone, usually by only a few degrees, but noticeably.
- **Dreams** — people who sleep near a stone-sleeper rock sometimes have unusual dreams. The content varies, but there is often a sense of **depth** — dreams of being underground, of vast slow movements, of things waking very slowly.

### Sites

Stone-sleeper rocks have been identified at:

- **The Heart of Brell** — a large dark stone in the Brell mining area. The local miners do not mine near it. The stone is said to breathe more strongly in autumn than in spring.
- **Carrick's Rest** in the upper Greyspine — a granite outcrop that hikers avoid.
- **The sealed sites beneath Underdam** — where the [Deep Vein](../kingdoms/veth.md#underdam) and the Halvers' Reach were closed. The stone-sleeper sounds from these sites are **not following the expected pattern**.

### Behaviour

Under normal conditions, stone-sleepers do nothing. They breathe. They sleep. They warm their stone. They have no apparent interest in the living world and no apparent awareness of it.

This may no longer be true of the [stone-sleepers beneath Underdam](phenomena.md#the-thing-under-underdam). Their sounds are now **louder, deeper, and occasionally modulated** in ways that resemble something other than breathing.

### Theories

- **Mineralised old entities** — creatures of the Old World that retreated into stone during the First Collapse and have remained there.
- **Natural phenomena** — simply unusual geological events that the human perceptual system interprets as living. Most scholars reject this.
- **The sleeping part of something larger** — the most disturbing view, held by some Architect scholars in private: that stone-sleepers are *parts* of something that, taken together, constitutes a vast sleeping entity spanning a wide area.

## The Drifting

> **Type:** Phenomenon, possibly living
> **Habitat:** Open water, usually at night
> **Aggression:** Ambiguous
> **Rarity:** Rare; increasing

A phenomenon observed by sailors in the [Bitter Reach](../world/overview.md#geography) and, more recently, in waters closer to the [Siltmere](../kingdoms/siltmere.md) coast.

### Description

The Drifting appears as a patch of water — typically a few yards across, sometimes larger — that is **differently coloured** from the surrounding sea. The colour varies: sometimes darker, sometimes lighter, sometimes faintly green, sometimes faintly yellow. The water within the patch moves differently than the surrounding water — currents flow in directions they should not, small waves rise where none should be.

**The Drifting follows ships.** Slowly. Patiently. A ship may notice a patch of strange water astern at dusk and find the same patch still astern at dawn. It does not approach. It follows.

Ships that are followed by the Drifting for multiple nights report: reduced appetite among the crew, restless sleep, unusual dreams, occasional visual distortions. No direct harm has been recorded.

Ships that **sail into** the Drifting deliberately have, on a small number of recorded occasions, found their wake oddly empty — as if something that had been following them is no longer there.

### Recent changes

The Drifting has been observed **closer to shore** than in prior generations. Some recent sightings have been in Siltmere harbours themselves, which is unprecedented.

The [crewless ships](phenomena.md#the-ship-incidents) that have returned to Siltmere ports were all, at some point in their voyages, **followed by the Drifting**. The connection has not been publicly made.

## Old Things

A category term rather than a specific creature. **"Old things"** refers, in Varekian folk speech, to entities that are rare, poorly understood, and understood to be very old. They include:

### The Long-Limbed

Tall, spindly figures occasionally seen at dusk in open country. Not humanoid. Not animal. Something in between. They do not approach settlements. They are seen standing, often at a distance, and then not seen again.

### The Quiet Companions

Small figures — perhaps a hand's height — reported in certain households. They do not harm. They occasionally move small objects. They are associated, in folk belief, with the [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md) and the [Undergods](../faiths/buried-faiths.md#the-undergods). Most households that have them keep them. They are not a problem.

### The Bone Riders

A rare sighting — perhaps one per decade — of what appear to be figures riding skeletal horses across open ground at night. Never approach settlements. Ride in consistent directions — always toward the [Hollow Lands](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-hollow-lands). The tradition holds that seeing a Bone Rider is unlucky but not fatal.

### The Open-Throated

A type of figure reported very rarely — perhaps three times in the last century. A humanoid shape with its throat **hanging open** as if a wound were there, but no blood, no apparent injury. They stand at crossroads at night. They do not speak. They do not move. They have never been approached closely. They are considered an omen of something specific — though what they are an omen of is not agreed upon.

## What is not a creature

A note: certain things that Varekians sometimes fear are not, properly, creatures.

- **The weather** is not a creature, though some storms in the Greyspine are severe enough to feel like one.
- **The Draining** is not a creature. It is a process.
- **The empty villages** were not emptied by a creature, so far as anyone has been able to determine. What emptied them did not leave tracks. See [Phenomena](phenomena.md).
- **The dreamers' plague** is not spread by a creature. It is not clear what spreads it.

## What is changing

The bestiary of Varek is, broadly, **stable over centuries**. Creatures appear, behave, and disappear in patterns that have been documented for generations.

But in the last five years, several things have shifted:

1. **Existing creatures appearing in new ranges.** Hollow-men in the Ashfields. Night-calves in the southern uplands. The Drifting in harbours.
2. **Existing creatures behaving in new ways.** Reader-birds with "false" readings. Stone-sleepers whose sounds are no longer purely breath.
3. **New phenomena with no clear creature associated.** See [Phenomena](phenomena.md).

The traditional bestiary does not fully account for what is now happening in Varek. New categories may be needed. The scholars who would develop those categories have not yet realised they are needed.
