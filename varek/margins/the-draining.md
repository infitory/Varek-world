# The Draining

> **Type:** Ongoing phenomenon / the slow dying of the world
> **Duration:** At least 80 years of measurement; likely centuries of reality
> **Status:** Canonical

The name given — mostly by scholars, occasionally by worried farmers — to whatever is happening to [Varek's](../world/overview.md) vitality.

## What the Draining is

The Draining is not dramatic. It is **statistical**. It consists of many small declines that, individually, could be explained by bad luck or poor management. Collectively, they describe the slow exhaustion of a world.

### Measurable effects

- **More infant deaths.** Child mortality has risen gradually across all four kingdoms over the last century. Current rates are roughly twenty percent higher than they were eighty years ago.
- **Smaller harvests.** Grain yields have declined by an estimated fifteen to twenty percent over the same period, varying by region. The [Ashfields](../world/regions.md#the-ashfields) are worst affected.
- **Smaller livestock.** Mature cattle today are, on average, noticeably smaller than their grandparents' generation. The difference is visible in old leather goods — old belts and harnesses are wider than modern equivalents, cut from larger animals.
- **Fewer songbirds.** The dawn chorus in most Varekian villages is quieter than older people remember. Some species that elders recall from childhood are no longer heard at all.
- **Deeper wells.** Water tables are dropping. Wells that served for centuries must now be re-dug deeper, or abandoned.
- **Slower healing.** Wounds, on average, take longer to close than they did in older medical records. Infections are more common. A minor injury in modern Varek is meaningfully more dangerous than the same injury would have been in the Old World.
- **More stillbirths.** Particularly in regions where the Draining is worst.
- **Certain plants diminishing.** Wild herbs and medicinal plants once common in particular regions have become rarer. Some are effectively extinct in some areas.
- **Longer winters.** By roughly two weeks, on average, compared to the records from a century ago.
- **Dimmer sunlight.** This is the most disputed claim. Some scholars argue that Varekian sunlight is less intense than it was in Old World accounts. Others believe this is literary exaggeration. The truth, most likely, is that something has shifted — but subtly enough that no one can prove it.

## When it began

The Draining's official measurement dates back approximately eighty years, to the work of an Architect scholar named **Brother Resselm of Varrentholm**, who first systematically documented the decline in agricultural yields across Ostmere.

The **phenomenon itself** is older. The Draining was likely present — perhaps subtly — throughout the [Long Reconstruction](../history/long-reconstruction.md). Some scholars argue it began at the [First Collapse](../history/first-collapse.md) and has simply continued for four centuries.

The most cautious assessment: **the Draining is at least as old as recorded measurement, and possibly far older**.

## Where it is worst

The Draining is not uniform. Some regions are more affected than others.

### Severely affected

- **The southern Ashfields** — where agricultural decline is most pronounced. Several villages have been abandoned in the last forty years because the land no longer supports them.
- **The Hollow Lands** — the worst-affected region in Varek. Effectively depopulated. The land there is not merely tired but *emptied*. See [The Hollow Lands](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-hollow-lands).
- **The upper Greyspine** — less productive than Ostmere, but the Draining shows in specific ways: wells running dry, certain mining seams producing less ore than they should given the extraction rate, unusually cold winters.

### Moderately affected

- **Most of Ostmere** — the Ashfields are the canonical example of moderate Draining. Harvests are smaller, children sicker, but life is still recognisably functional.
- **Most of Veth** — similar to the northern Ashfields, with the additional factor of the already-harsh mountain conditions.
- **The Vorrlands** — the [Vorrwood](../world/regions.md#the-vorrlands) itself may be less affected than the cultivated land around it. The deep forest seems, in some ways, **more alive** than it should be. This is its own concern.

### Less affected

- **The Siltmere coast** — curiously. The [Siltmere ports](../kingdoms/siltmere.md) have experienced less apparent Draining than inland regions. Fish populations remain reasonably stable. Agriculture in the coastal lowlands has declined, but less sharply than in the Ashfields. Some scholars hypothesise that the sea itself is less susceptible, though the recent [ship incidents](phenomena.md) suggest this may be changing.
- **Certain high valleys in the Greyspine** — isolated pockets where the Draining appears to have been slower. No one is quite sure why.

## The rate

The Draining has been neither accelerating sharply nor reversing. It **simply continues**.

Most people do not notice it in their own lifetimes. The effect is too slow. What they notice is that their grandparents' stories seem exaggerated. The wheat was never that tall. The birds were never that many. The winters were never that short. It must have been nostalgia.

Those who live long enough, and pay attention, do notice. Old farmers. Old clergy. Old scholars. They know.

## Explanations

Everyone has a theory. Few are satisfying.

### The Exhaustion Theory

Most secular scholars favour some version of the Exhaustion Theory (the same theory that applies to the [First Collapse](../history/first-collapse.md#the-exhaustion-theory)): the Old World drained the world of some vital substance, and what remains is slowly running out. The Draining, in this view, is the continued depletion of whatever was already being depleted.

This theory has the virtue of consistency. It also has the flaw of not identifying what the substance is, why it was being depleted, or whether anything can be done to stop it.

### The Punishment Theory

The [Six-Faced clergy's](../faiths/six-faced-god.md) official position: the Draining is the ongoing consequence of humanity's earlier transgression. The [Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound) order treats the Draining as spiritually instructive — suffering that teaches. The [Judge](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-judge) order treats it as a verdict still being executed.

### The Starving-Gods Theory

A view held by practitioners of the [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md) and certain adherents of the [Buried Faiths](../faiths/buried-faiths.md): the local gods and spirits that once sustained the land are **being starved**. By what, is disputed. Some say by the displacement caused by the Six-Faced faith. Some say by something that feeds on them. Some say by the simple withdrawal of the belief and attention that sustained them.

### The Slow Predation Theory

A disturbing variant of the Intrusion Theory. This view holds that **something entered Varek at the First Collapse and has been slowly consuming it ever since** — not dramatically, but over centuries. The Draining, in this view, is the evidence of an ongoing feed. The world is not dying naturally. It is being eaten.

The [Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md) consider this theory possible. They do not promote it. It is considered a fringe view, though not by those who hold it.

### The Completion Theory

A rare view, held by very few: that the Draining is not a symptom of dysfunction but the **expected outcome** of a process the Old World began. That the Collapse initiated something, and the Draining is the long tail of its completion — the slow, steady working-out of whatever was set in motion.

This theory is associated with scholars who have read deeply in the fragments the [Reclamation](../history/long-reconstruction.md#the-reclamation) missed. They do not publish. They do not discuss their view openly. It is known only by implication.

## The psychological effect

The Draining affects more than harvests. It affects the **mood** of Varek.

Varekian culture is characterised by:

- **A pervasive mild pessimism.** Not despair — pessimism. The default expectation is that things will be slightly worse this year than last, and considerably worse a generation from now.
- **A reverence for the past that is also a grief for it.** Every Varekian grows up knowing that the world was richer once. Many develop a complicated relationship with this knowledge — a longing for a world they never lived in, which also means a diminished investment in the world they do live in.
- **A distrust of hope.** Hope, in Varek, is widely considered a mild moral failing — a form of laziness or delusion. "Sensible" people do not hope. They endure.
- **A particular humour.** Dry, dark, often at the expense of the hopeful. Ostmere folk-sayings include "*Better to dig the grave while you can still hold the shovel*" and "*The wheat will come up, or it won't, and we have no say either way*."

Whether this culture is a response to the Draining or a cause of its perception is not clear. Probably both. The Draining makes pessimism rational; pessimism makes the Draining easier to bear.

## The Draining and Aldric

[Aldric](../characters/aldric.md) is, in a literal sense, **a personal instance of the Draining**. He has the still-cough. His body is slowly wasting in the same way the world is. His left hand trembles. His chest tightens. He is dying at the rate of a few percent per year — the same rate, roughly, at which the Ashfields around him are dying.

Aldric does not think of it this way. But the parallel is there.

He chose his chapel in the eastern Ashfields — one of the moderately drained regions. The land around him is tired. He is tired. The world and the man are, for now, dying together at the same pace.

Something is about to change that.

## The recent shift

In the last five years, alongside the ongoing slow Draining, a **different kind of pattern** has emerged. Not a slow decline. A series of **acute anomalies**.

- The [empty villages in the Vorrwood](phenomena.md).
- The sealed mines beneath [Underdam](../kingdoms/veth.md#underdam).
- The crewless ships returning to the [Siltmere](../kingdoms/siltmere.md).
- The [Long Walker](phenomena.md) in the Ashfields.
- The [dreamers' plague](phenomena.md).
- The increased response rate of the [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md).
- The withdrawal of the [Unnamed's](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-unnamed) followers.

These are not the Draining. They are something else.

The question — unanswered, unasked by most — is whether they are **caused** by the Draining, or whether they are **replacing** it: the slow decline giving way to something faster, more active, and with an agenda.
