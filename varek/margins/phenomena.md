# Recent Phenomena

> **Type:** Anomalous events of the current era
> **Period:** The last 1-5 years
> **Status:** Canonical — and the core of what is happening

The events that, collectively, indicate that something has changed in [Varek](../world/overview.md). These phenomena are **not explained** by the traditional bestiary of [creatures](creatures.md), by the slow attrition of the [Draining](the-draining.md), or by any recognised theological framework.

They are also **not connected** in anyone's mind yet. Each is known locally. No single person in Varek has seen the whole pattern.

---

## The Empty Villages

> **Location:** The [Vorrwood](../world/regions.md#the-vorrlands), near [Dusk's Edge](../kingdoms/free-cities.md#dusks-edge)
> **First incident:** Approximately 8 months ago
> **Most recent:** 3 weeks ago
> **Total affected:** 3 villages, 107 missing inhabitants

Three villages in the Vorrwood, all within a month's journey of Dusk's Edge, have been found empty. No bodies. No signs of struggle. Doors open. Livestock still penned. Food still on tables.

### Halt-by-the-Wash
The first. A logging village of thirty-four inhabitants, found empty in early spring. A trader arriving to pick up a contracted load of lumber found the village normal except that no one was there. Meals were on tables. Some hearths still warm. Nothing was missing except the people — no sign of robbery, no missing valuables, no abandoned half-packed bags.

The trader reported the discovery to Dusk's Edge. An investigation was dispatched. It found nothing. No tracks leading out of the village. No bodies in the surrounding forest. The livestock were eventually redistributed to nearby settlements. The village has been officially abandoned.

### Old Ferris
The second. Fifty-one inhabitants, emptied in late summer. Similar pattern to Halt-by-the-Wash. One distinctive difference: **one of the cooking pots was still boiling** when the first visitors arrived. The fire had been tended within, at most, half a day before. Whatever happened was recent enough that the fire had not gone out.

An investigation spent four days in Old Ferris. On the fifth day, one investigator did not return from a routine perimeter check. The others left the village that night and did not return. The missing investigator has not been found. His name was **Heller Vresh**, a Dusk's Edge militia officer, aged thirty-one, with a wife and two children.

### Crane's Hollow
The third. Emptied three weeks ago. Twenty-two inhabitants. Smaller than the others.

**The village's bell was still ringing** when the investigating party arrived — no wind, no visible cause. It rang for an hour after they arrived. Then it stopped. Nothing was touching it. No one saw it stop.

The investigating party left within the hour. They have not been willing to discuss what they saw or felt. One of them has since requested a transfer out of the militia. Another has taken a short silence with the [Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md) and will not speak for another two months.

### Pattern

The three villages are **spaced roughly evenly apart**, forming a rough line that extends deeper into the Vorrwood. If the pattern continues, the next affected village is predictable: **Low Harvenn**, a logging and fur-trading settlement of roughly forty inhabitants, approximately three weeks' travel from Crane's Hollow.

The [High Chapter of Dusk's Edge](../kingdoms/free-cities.md#dusks-edge) has dispatched three separate investigations into the Vorrwood. **None have returned.** A fourth is being organised, slowly, because volunteers are becoming difficult to find.

### What is known

Nothing.

### What is suspected

- Something is taking the villages. Not killing. Not robbing. **Taking.**
- The investigations that have disappeared were also taken — apparently in the same manner.
- Whatever is doing this is moving deeper into the Vorrwood, or summoning from it.
- The bell at Crane's Hollow is the only point in the pattern where the taker left a signal, deliberate or not. Why, is not clear.

## The Sealed Mines of Underdam

> **Location:** [Underdam](../kingdoms/veth.md#underdam), in [Veth](../kingdoms/veth.md)
> **First incident:** Late winter (roughly 10 months ago)
> **Status:** Actively developing

Two mines have been sealed in the last year:

- **The Deep Vein** — sealed approximately 10 months ago.
- **The Halvers' Reach** — sealed approximately 4 months ago.

In both cases, workers reported **sounds from below** — slow, rhythmic, at frequencies far too low to be ordinary. A few workers described the sounds becoming *intelligible*, as if almost-words.

### The progression

In both mines, the phenomenon developed in similar phases:

1. **The breathing phase** (initial 1-2 months) — the sound is clearly a breathing pattern. Slow. Deep. Roughly four to six breaths per hour. Workers note it but continue working.
2. **The modulation phase** (2-3 months in) — the breathing begins to vary. Sometimes faster. Sometimes deeper. Patterns emerge that do not match breathing.
3. **The speaking phase** (3-6 months in) — the sounds begin to resemble words. Not any recognisable language. But the **structure** of speech — pauses, groupings, something that sounds like emphasis.
4. **The specific phase** (6+ months in) — workers begin reporting that the sounds are saying **specific things**. Usually their own names. Or the names of the dead. Or statements in what sounds like the workers' own voices.

Mines have been sealed at the early specific phase in both cases.

### The workers

Workers exposed to the phenomenon have fared poorly:
- Two workers of the Deep Vein have died. The causes were not investigated. One fell from a bridge. One was found in his bed with no visible cause of death.
- Three workers of the Halvers' Reach have gone silent. Not [Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md)-silent. Simply stopped speaking. They continue to eat, to work, to move about their lives. They do not speak.
- A small number of workers from both mines have requested reassignment and have been quietly relocated. They are being kept track of by Underdam's administration but are not being openly watched.

### The sounds continuing

**The sounds have not stopped with the sealing.** They are now audible:

- Through the floor of the mine offices on the surface.
- In certain cellars of nearby buildings.
- Occasionally — recently — at the edge of a street in the city's lower district.

This has not been made public. The city administration is attempting to contain the information. They are failing; rumours are spreading in Underdam. Within a few more weeks, this will be common knowledge in the city. Within a few months, it will be unstoppable.

### What Veth knows

**Iron Captain Havel Korrin** has been personally briefed on the Underdam situation by its city governor. He has not yet informed the Prince-Marshal. He is trying to decide how to frame it.

The [Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound) clergy in Underdam have begun their own investigation. They have not shared their findings with the civil authorities.

## The Ship Incidents

> **Location:** [Siltmere Ports](../kingdoms/siltmere.md) (Vaun, Keth, Ord)
> **Timing:** Over the past 12 months
> **Total:** 3 ships so far

Three trading ships have returned to Siltmere ports **empty of crew**. Cargo intact. Logs ending in various ways.

### The Hessal's Virtue (Vaun, 6 months ago)
A grain ship returning from a southern trading route. Logs end mid-voyage, mid-sentence, in an entry about **"a colour the captain had not previously seen."** The ship's wheel was tied off. The sails were furled. The ship was sailing itself — steadily — home.

### The Sallowmark (Keth, 4 months ago)
A textile ship on a standard run. Logs end normally, but the next day's entry is written in a different hand and says only **"they have not come back."** The entries then stop. The ship was crewed by eleven. The log was not continued.

### The Dawn-Sister (Ord, 2 months ago)
A general cargo ship on a coastal route. Logs end with a week of normal entries, followed by **three pages of carefully drawn geometric patterns**, followed by blank pages. The patterns are not any known script. They are precise, deliberate, and possibly meaningful.

### The connection not yet made

**Each Council has suppressed news of its own incident.** None yet realise the other two have had identical ones.

This is about to change. Lady [Merris Tavell](../kingdoms/siltmere.md#vaun) of Vaun has heard rumours. She has instructed a trusted agent to investigate Keth. The agent has not yet reported back, but is expected within the week.

The patterns in the Dawn-Sister's log are in the possession of the Ord Harbour Council. **Lord-Merchant Halden Vorr** — the uncompromised chair — has had them examined by every scholar he trusts. One of those scholars has recognised **partial similarities** to Old World script but cannot translate.

When the three incidents are connected — and they will be — the Siltmere will know it has a problem that cannot be handled by any one port alone.

## The Long Walker

> **Location:** The [Ashfields](../world/regions.md#the-ashfields), various
> **First sighting:** Approximately 18 months ago
> **Current status:** Ongoing, periodic sightings

A figure reported in the Ashfields in the last eighteen months. Walks across fields at twilight. Tall. Thin. Always in the same direction — **north-east**. Does not speak. Does not respond. Has been seen by perhaps forty people.

### Description

Each description matches. Tall — at least seven feet, perhaps more. Thin to the point of appearing starved. Walking with a slow, steady pace — not hurrying, not meandering, simply walking. The walk never varies in pace, even when the Walker crosses uneven ground.

The Walker wears **clothing** — described as dark, loose, and old. A cloak. A hood. Its face has never been clearly seen. Those who have been close enough to see its face report that **they cannot later remember what it looked like**, though they are certain they saw it.

### Behaviour

The Walker does not interact. Those who have called out to it have had no response. Those who have tried to block its path have found the Walker simply steps around them without acknowledging. Those who have attempted to touch it report: the touch was cold, and they immediately regretted doing so, though they could not say why.

The Walker does not deviate from its direction. It walks **north-east**. When terrain prevents this — a building, a hill, a river — it goes around the obstacle in the shortest path and resumes its original direction.

Its pace is roughly that of a purposeful human walk. It has never been observed to rest. It has never been observed to speed up. It has never been observed at any time of day other than dusk — always the last hour of light, always just before full dark.

### The direction

North-east from the central Ashfields points toward: the northern reaches of the [Greyspine](../world/regions.md#the-greyspine) and, beyond them, the [Hollow Lands](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-hollow-lands).

The Walker is, apparently, walking **toward the Hollow Lands**.

It has been seen perhaps seventy miles from the Hollow Lands' nominal border. At its observed pace — and if it walks even one hour per day — it should reach the border within two to three months.

Has been walking, apparently, for at least eighteen months. Has covered at least several hundred miles. Its origin is not known. Where exactly it is coming from is not known. Whether it is the same Walker each time, or multiple Walkers, is not known.

Most observers assume a single Walker. They may be wrong.

## The Dreamers' Plague

> **Location:** Scattered villages across [Ostmere](../kingdoms/ostmere.md), some in [Veth](../kingdoms/veth.md), a few in the [Siltmere](../kingdoms/siltmere.md)
> **First reported:** Approximately 2 years ago
> **Current status:** Spreading slowly

Not a physical plague. Over the last two years, in scattered villages across Varek, people have begun reporting **the same dream**.

### The dream

The content of the dream is consistent across all reports:

- The dreamer is in a **long grey hall**.
- The hall is longer than seems possible — the far end is not visible, or is only visible as a kind of point.
- At the far end, or at the limit of vision, is a **door**.
- Behind the door, there is a **sound**.
- The sound is not clearly heard. It is muffled by the door.
- The dreamer is deeply grateful that the door is closed — that the sound is muffled, that the sound is kept separate.
- The dreamer does not approach the door. The dreamer does not want to approach the door.
- The dream ends when the dreamer wakes, usually suddenly, and usually with the sense that **something behind the door noticed them**.

### Patterns

- Different villages. Different people. The same dream.
- The dreamers do not know each other. Most have never met.
- The dream does not usually recur — most dreamers have it only once.
- **Some, however, do** have it again. Those who have it repeatedly have not been doing well.

### The repeat dreamers

A small fraction — perhaps one in fifteen of the dreamers — have the dream more than once. Their pattern is consistent:

- Second dream: the hall is slightly shorter. The door is slightly closer. The sound is slightly more audible.
- Third dream: the dreamer is aware, in the dream, that they are walking toward the door. They do not want to. They walk anyway.
- Fourth dream: they are very close to the door. Some stop dreaming the dream at this point. Some continue.
- Fifth dream (in the cases where it occurs): the dreamer is at the door.
- Sixth dream: reported only twice. In both cases, the dreamer was at the door, and the door was opening.

**Both of the sixth-dream dreamers have subsequently died.** In both cases, the cause of death was unclear. In one case, the dreamer simply did not wake up. In the other, the dreamer woke up, went about their morning normally, and then stopped breathing while eating breakfast, with no apparent cause.

### How it spreads

The dream does not appear to require contact. It does not follow rivers. It does not follow roads. It appears, more or less, to **spread like weather** — roughly in the direction of prevailing winds, but only roughly.

Recent outbreaks have been concentrated in:
- The northern Ashfields (early).
- The western Greyspine foothills (six months later).
- The southern Vorrlands (ongoing).
- Most recently: isolated cases in [Vaun](../kingdoms/siltmere.md#vaun).

The [Quiet](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-quiet) clergy are the ones who first recognised the dream as a pattern, because dying people began describing it with disturbing consistency. They have been documenting it, quietly. Their records are the best single source on the phenomenon. Their records have not been shared with any secular authority.

## The Quieting

> **Location:** Around the [empty villages in the Vorrwood](#the-empty-villages)
> **First observed:** During the Halt-by-the-Wash investigation
> **Current status:** Ongoing, expanding

A phenomenon observed in the [Vorrwood](../world/regions.md#the-vorrlands) around the sites of the empty villages. Within a certain radius of each emptied site, **animals do not live anymore**.

### Description

Not just absent — **silent**. The areas around Halt-by-the-Wash, Old Ferris, and Crane's Hollow have, in descending order of size, no birds, no insects, no small mammals, no frogs, no fish. Trees are still alive. Plants are still growing. Fungi are still present.

But the *sounds* of a forest — bird calls, insect hum, the distant movement of animals — are **entirely absent**.

Investigators report that the quieted areas are deeply unsettling to spend time in. The silence is not peaceful. It is **aware**. Several investigators describe a sense of being observed, though nothing is visibly there.

### Expansion

The quieted areas are **expanding**. Each is growing at a rate of approximately a hundred feet per month.

The area around Halt-by-the-Wash — the oldest — is now roughly two miles across. Old Ferris is approximately a mile and a half. Crane's Hollow is roughly half a mile and growing.

At the current expansion rate, the quieted areas will merge within a year.

### What does the quieting

Not known. The animals do not appear to be **killed** — they simply **leave**. Animals approaching a quieted area turn away. Animals already within one when it forms, apparently, leave.

Whether the quieting is caused by the same thing that took the villages, or whether it is a separate phenomenon that simply co-occurs, is not clear.

## The Thing Under Underdam

See [The Sealed Mines](#the-sealed-mines-of-underdam) above. The sounds continue. The sealed rock is not containing them. Whatever is beneath is closer to the surface, in some sense, than it was three months ago.

No visual contact has ever been made.

A [Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound) priest in Underdam has begun lowering a plumb-line with a bell on it into an air-shaft that connects to the deep workings. She does this every night at the same hour. She has not yet reported what, if anything, responds.

## The Withdrawal of the Unnamed

See [The Unnamed](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-unnamed) in the Six-Faced God entry.

The followers of the Unnamed have, in the last year, gone almost entirely silent. Their shrines are dismantled. Their meetings have ceased. Adherents who were once willing to discuss the face privately now refuse.

A few have left their homes without explanation.

**This has happened before**, to a lesser degree — during the Silent Schism thirty-one years ago. The current withdrawal is different. It is broader. It is quieter. It is more complete.

The [Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md) regard the Unnamed's silence as significant. They will not say more.

## The Increase in the Old Rites' Answers

See [The Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md#the-recent-increase-in-detail).

The response rate of the Old Rites has roughly doubled in the last five years. What is answering is not known. That something is answering, and answering more often, is now common knowledge among the older women of rural Varek.

## The Long Walker, continued

The Long Walker, described above, will reach the Hollow Lands within a few months at current pace.

**Nobody knows what will happen when it arrives.**

It is possible nothing will. It is possible the Walker simply crosses into the Hollow Lands and continues, and is never seen again. It is possible it arrives and stops, having reached whatever destination it was walking toward. It is possible it arrives and **something else begins**.

The Watchmen of the Edge at [Harrow's Reach](../kingdoms/ostmere.md#harrows-reach) have been informed of the Walker's approach by a passing traveller. They have made notes. They are watching the border. They have not shared their observations beyond their own records.

## The pattern, if anyone could see it

Taken individually, each of these phenomena has a local explanation or a local scope. Taken together, they form a pattern:

- Something is **taking** people in the Vorrwood.
- Something is **speaking** beneath Underdam.
- Something is **travelling** toward the Hollow Lands.
- Something is **dreaming into** Varek from elsewhere.
- Something is **quieting** places where its effect is strongest.
- Something that used to be silent in the faith structure is **more silent**.
- Something that the Old Rites used to invoke is **answering more often**.

**There is no central authority that is aware of the full pattern.** The High Chapter of Dusk's Edge knows about the empty villages. Underdam knows about the mines. The Siltmere Councils each know about their own ships. The Six-Faced clergy know about the Unnamed's silence. The Voiceless know about all of this in fragments.

No one has connected it all.

Someone needs to. The person who does may not be a king or a priest or a scholar. The person who does may be someone who simply ends up, through no merit of their own, in the right places at the right times.

Perhaps a soldier. Perhaps a dying one.
