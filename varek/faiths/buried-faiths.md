# The Buried Faiths

> **Type:** Heretical and minor religions
> **Status:** Canonical

Varek has other gods. Most of them are dead, or asleep, or officially non-existent. All of them have followers.

---

## The Drowned Mothers

> **Type:** Regional cult
> **Followers:** Perhaps one in twenty rural households in river country
> **Where strongest:** [Siltmere](../kingdoms/siltmere.md), [Vorrlands](../kingdoms/free-cities.md), river valleys in Ostmere

A pre-collapse river-cult that survives in fragments. Its core belief: **the rivers of Varek are the bodies of drowned goddesses**, and must be propitiated with small offerings and certain songs.

### Doctrine

The Drowned Mothers were, in the telling, three sister-goddesses who descended into the rivers of Varek in the time before the [First Collapse](../history/first-collapse.md). Their names vary by region:

- In the Siltmere they are **Veshira, Morlune, and Thress**.
- In the Vorrlands they are **the Mother of the Wend, the Mother of the Vorr, and the Mother of the Little Brack** — named after the three rivers of Verak's Mouth.
- In the Ashfields, where the cult is weakest, they are simply **the Sisters**.

They descended willingly. Why they did is the central mystery of the faith. The answers vary: to hide something, to contain something, to grieve something, to become something.

They are said to still be in the rivers, diminished but present. Offerings to the rivers reach them. Songs sung from riverbanks at certain times are heard by them.

### Practices

- **River offerings** — small items cast into moving water. Flowers. Baked bread. Carved wooden figures. Coins only in the Siltmere, where the cult has become commercialised.
- **The Mother's Songs** — traditional songs, sung on specific days, only by women, at riverbanks. The songs are in an older form of Varekian that many singers do not fully understand.
- **The First Bath** — a newborn child is bathed in river-water within three days of birth. The water is drawn from a specific stretch of river, with a specific invocation.
- **The Mother's Hand** — a hand-shaped charm carried by many cult members, worn as a pendant or sewn into clothing. Made from river-stone, river-wood, or (in older variants) from a bone found in a river, cause of origin not asked.

### Relationship with other faiths

The [Six-Faced clergy](six-faced-god.md) officially disapprove of the Drowned Mothers cult but rarely prosecute it. The cult is woven so deeply into river-country practice that eradicating it would require prosecuting a significant fraction of rural households.

The cult does not conflict openly with the [Old Rites](old-rites.md); indeed, many practitioners of the Drowned Mothers consider their cult to be a formalised branch of the Old Rites.

### Recent developments

The Drowned Mothers' priestesses — the older women who hold the cult's songs and practices — have reported, in the last year, that **the rivers feel different**. The offerings, they say, are being received differently. The songs are being answered more often. Not in words. In weather. In the behaviour of fish. In the movement of water.

Two priestesses on the river Wend, in the Free Cities, have reported that the river's voice has become audible to them — not loud, not clear, but present in a way it was not before. They are not telling anyone outside the cult.

## The Hollow King

> **Type:** Heretical faith
> **Followers:** Perhaps 2,000, concentrated in the Ashfields and the Broken Counties
> **Status:** Actively suppressed by the Six-Faced clergy

A heretical figure considered by his followers to be the **true god of Varek** — the god the Six-Faced God was designed to hide.

### Doctrine

The Hollow King is described as **the god beneath the god**. The Six-Faced God, to the Hollow King's followers, is not a lie but a **veil** — a deliberate construction, developed during the [Long Reconstruction](../history/long-reconstruction.md), intended to prevent humanity from remembering the true god.

Why the true god was veiled is disputed among his followers. Some say the Hollow King demanded a hiding for his own purposes. Some say the early [Architect](six-faced-god.md#the-architect) clergy of the Reconstruction discovered him and decided humanity could not bear him. Some say the Hollow King was punished by older gods and the Six-Faced God was installed in his place as the punishment itself.

### Who the Hollow King is

His followers describe him variously:

- A king who lost his throne.
- A king who never had a throne.
- A king who is the throne, and the land beneath it.
- A king who is empty — whose crown sits above a space where a head should be.

The imagery of the **Empty Crown** and the **Unoccupied Throne** appears in Hollow King iconography. His symbol is a crown with nothing beneath it, usually drawn in ash or charcoal on hidden walls.

### Practices

Hollow King worship is furtive. It involves:

- **Night gatherings** in the ruins of old buildings, particularly [Old World](../history/old-world.md) ruins.
- **The reading of the Black Scroll** — a text compiled in fragments over centuries, considered heretical by the Six-Faced clergy. Possession of a copy is a serious offence in Ostmere and Veth. There is no single authoritative version; copies differ, sometimes considerably.
- **The Empty Communion** — a ritual meal in which an empty place is set for the Hollow King. Food is served and left untouched. The meal is taken in silence.
- **The Naming of Ruins** — a practice in which members recite the names (known or invented) of places the Old World has lost. The practice is said to draw the Hollow King's attention briefly.

### Why the Six-Faced clergy take them seriously

Most ordinary people in Ostmere and Veth regard the Hollow King cult as a fringe heresy, barely worth concern.

**The Six-Faced clergy do not.** Particularly at senior levels, the Architect and Judge orders treat the Hollow King as a genuine threat. They hunt his followers with more energy than any other heresy. Possession of a Black Scroll is punished severely. Meetings, when discovered, are broken up with violence that exceeds what the crime seems to warrant.

The reason is not publicly shared. But in the private archives of the Architect order — in the deep archives no junior scribe is permitted — there are documents from the [Reclamation](../history/long-reconstruction.md#the-reclamation) that describe what was done during that period and why. The documents describe the **deliberate burial** of something that had been found in the Old World ruins — a presence, a figure, a truth — and the construction of the Six-Faced faith as a structure designed, in part, to prevent its re-emergence.

Whether the buried presence is the Hollow King, or whether the Hollow King cult is simply a corrupted memory of the burial, is not clear even in the deep archive.

What is clear: the senior Architect clergy consider the Hollow King's re-emergence to be one of the worst possible outcomes for Varek. They do not discuss why.

## The Pale Twins

> **Type:** Small regional cult
> **Followers:** A few hundred, almost entirely in the Greyspine
> **Status:** Tolerated

A cult of two figures, described only as siblings — **the Pale Twins**. Their adherents practise ritual fasting and a form of divination by shadow.

### Doctrine

The Pale Twins are described as:
- A brother and sister.
- Very old.
- Not quite alive, not quite dead.
- Concerned with the threshold between states — day and night, waking and sleeping, life and death.

They are neither worshipped nor appeased in the conventional sense. They are **consulted**.

### Practices

- **The Fast of the Shadow** — a three-day fast culminating in a period of observation at dusk. The practitioner watches their own shadow in fading light and reports what the shadow does.
- **The Whispering** — a practice in which questions are spoken at twilight, in specific words, and the answer is said to appear in the practitioner's dreams that same night.
- **The Twinning** — a practice where two practitioners perform the same ritual in separate rooms simultaneously and then compare what happened. Consistent results are considered meaningful.

### Reputation

Largely dismissed by mainstream Varekian culture. Considered by those who know of them to be a harmless eccentricity of certain Greyspine communities. The Six-Faced clergy do not prosecute them — they are too small and too weird to matter.

The cult has been stable in numbers for centuries. It does not seek converts. It does not proselytise. Its members tend to be born into it or to find it through some personal event.

## The Undergods

> **Type:** Category of folk beliefs
> **Status:** Widespread; semi-canonical

Not a specific faith. A category. The belief, widespread in agricultural communities, that there are **local gods tied to specific features of the land** — a particular hill, a particular spring, a particular tree.

The Undergods are not named in any universal way. They are simply **acknowledged, regionally**.

### Examples

- **The Stone of Brell** — a large dark stone near the mining town of [Brell](../kingdoms/ostmere.md#brell). Miners touch it before descending. No one has moved it in living memory.
- **The Drinking Pine** — a particular pine tree in a specific Vorrland grove. Water poured at its base is said to return as a good dream within three nights.
- **The Grey Hill** near [Drost's Fief](../kingdoms/ostmere.md#drosts-fief) — not to be built upon. Not to be ploughed. The reason is lost but the prohibition is absolute.
- **The Old Spring** in the southern Ashfields — said to have spoken, once, to a specific woman, who kept what it said to herself.

### Theology

The Undergods are not worshipped as the Six-Faced God is worshipped. They are **respected**. Small offerings are left. Certain behaviours are observed. Certain prohibitions are honoured. Nothing elaborate.

Most of the Undergods are probably real in some sense — at least, in the sense that something is there, and something responds. Most of them are also almost certainly **weakening with the [Draining](../margins/the-draining.md)**.

### The fading

Older people in rural Varek often report that particular Undergods have **gone quiet**. A spring that used to answer no longer does. A stone that used to be warm is now cold. A tree that used to make the air around it feel different now feels like any other tree.

Some communities perform ceremonies to recall their Undergods. These ceremonies, by and large, do not seem to work. But a small number have — and the Undergods that have responded after decades of silence have responded **differently** than they did before.

In one Ashfield village — the name is not widely shared — a local Undergod was called back after forty years of quiet. The village's oldest woman led the ceremony. When the Undergod responded, it **spoke**. In Varekian. Clearly. At length.

The village has not shared what it said. The old woman has not spoken since the ceremony — not as a vow, but because she seems no longer able to.

## The Dying

> **Type:** Rumoured doctrine
> **Followers:** Unclear — a handful, if any
> **Status:** Nearly mythical

A doctrine that may or may not have adherents. Its belief: that all the gods of Varek are **dying**, and have been dying for a long time, and that the proper response to this fact is neither worship nor resistance but **witness**.

The Dying are rumoured to exist. They are described as small groups, meeting in places of death — graveyards, battlefields, plague-houses — to observe and record the ongoing decline of the divine.

Whether they actually exist, whether they are a thought-experiment that some philosopher proposed and some writers repeated, whether the Six-Faced clergy invented them as a straw doctrine to be condemned, is not known.

If they exist, they are rare enough that no one has credibly claimed to have met one.

The [Voiceless](voiceless.md) acknowledge the Dying as a possibility and neither endorse nor condemn the doctrine. The Six-Faced clergy condemn them formally and rarely mention them otherwise.

The doctrine is mentioned here because it may become relevant. Or it may not. It is the kind of thing that, in a world like Varek, should be noted in case it matters later.
