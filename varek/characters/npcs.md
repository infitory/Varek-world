# Other Named Figures

> **Type:** Characters (non-player / supporting)
> **Status:** Canonical

A catalogue of named figures who exist in [Varek](../world/overview.md) as of the current year. They may or may not enter [Aldric's](aldric.md) story. They exist regardless.

---

## Rulers and high officials

### King Edvar III Varrent
> **Location:** Varrentholm, Ostmere
> **Age:** 61
> **Role:** King of [Ostmere](../kingdoms/ostmere.md)

Sixty-one years old. In reasonable health. Without a clear successor. Not cruel, not corrupt, not particularly capable. Maintains order through inertia. His bureaucracy functions without requiring him to do much.

His son died at eleven. His daughter, Princess Iselle, is twenty-four and unmarried. He has not solved the succession problem. He does not appear to be trying.

### Princess Iselle Varrent
> **Location:** Varrentholm, Ostmere
> **Age:** 24
> **Role:** Heir apparent (contested)

The only legitimate child of King Edvar. Twenty-four. Unmarried. Considered by the court to be intelligent, private, and politically passive — a combination that alarms the senior lords, who would prefer a more active princess whose alliances they could exploit or resist.

Iselle reads extensively. She corresponds with several scholars of the [Architect order](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect), including High Speaker Ormund Vale. She has shown particular interest in Old World history — an interest that, in the last year, has intensified. She has requested access to certain archived materials. High Speaker Vale has, for now, refused.

She suspects her father will not choose a marriage for her, and that she will inherit, unmarried, a kingdom that does not wish to have her.

### High Speaker Ormund Vale
> **Location:** Varrentholm, Ostmere
> **Age:** 67
> **Role:** High Speaker of [The Architect](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect); Crown Voice of the Six Faces for Ostmere

The senior [Architect](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect) priest of Ostmere and one of the most politically significant people in Varek.

Ormund has access to the deep archive beneath the Grand Seminary. In the last four months, he has begun reading portions of it he had previously avoided. He is not sure why he chose now. The material is disturbing him.

He has not yet decided whether to share what he is learning with anyone. He has considered Princess Iselle as a potential confidante. He has considered resigning his position.

### Lord Marshal Vannis Therrick
> **Location:** Varrentholm, Ostmere
> **Age:** 72
> **Role:** Senior military commander of Ostmere

The architect of the final Ostmere offensive in the [Sixteen-Year War](../history/veth-wars.md). Now Lord Marshal. Aged but still serving — the kind of man who will die in his office chair.

Therrick knew Aldric's unit commander well. He approved Aldric's promotion to captain. If told that Aldric had left the army, Therrick would not be surprised but would be mildly disappointed. He had considered Aldric a potential successor to Drost's role in a few years.

### Prince-Marshal Serak Veth IV
> **Location:** Serrath, Veth
> **Age:** 48
> **Role:** Ruler of [Veth](../kingdoms/veth.md)

Inherited the principality from his father twenty-two years ago. Argued against his father's war. Is now, for reasons he would not have predicted, considering whether to start one himself.

Serak is intelligent, cornered, and exhausted. His wife Valleya is his main political counsel. His Coin-Steward Haela Drev is his main economic one. Neither of them is advising war. His military commanders are.

### Princess-Consort Valleya
> **Location:** Serrath, Veth
> **Age:** 56
> **Role:** Wife of the Prince-Marshal; effective co-ruler

Eight years older than her husband. The more politically capable of the two. Advises him privately; has her own network of contacts across the kingdoms; is widely respected as a diplomat and feared as a strategist.

She is opposed to a fourth Veth war. She is losing the argument within the War Council.

### Coin-Steward Haela Drev
> **Location:** Serrath, Veth
> **Age:** 51
> **Role:** Master of the Veth treasury

Widely considered the most terrifying person in Veth. Not because she is cruel — she is not. Because she knows exactly what everyone owes, and she does not forget.

Haela has presented the Prince-Marshal with a detailed accounting of what a fourth war would cost. The numbers do not support the proposal. She has made this clear in writing. She has been ignored so far.

### Lady Merris Tavell
> **Location:** Vaun, Siltmere
> **Age:** 40
> **Role:** First Speaker of the Council of Nine, [Vaun](../kingdoms/siltmere.md#vaun)

Inherited her position after her father's assassination. Has ruled the Council of Nine for seven years. Politically skilled, economically ruthless, personally reserved.

Merris has noticed the [Hessal's Virtue](../margins/phenomena.md#the-ship-incidents) incident and has asked about it. She has not yet connected it to the Siltmere-wide pattern. She is the most likely person in Vaun to make that connection when the pattern becomes clearer.

### Warden Ildeth Korr
> **Location:** Hollfen, Free Cities (recently unseen)
> **Age:** 54
> **Role:** Warden of [Hollfen](../kingdoms/free-cities.md#hollfen)

Has not been seen in public for fourteen days. This is not yet unusual for her — she is a private ruler — but it is beginning to be noticed. The Council of Shades speaks for her. What they speak for her is, lately, slightly different than it was.

Whether Ildeth is ill, incapacitated, away, dead, or something else, is not known outside the innermost Hollfen circle. Possibly not known even there.

### Speaker Arven Del
> **Location:** Dusk's Edge, Free Cities
> **Age:** 45
> **Role:** First Voice of the High Chapter of [Dusk's Edge](../kingdoms/free-cities.md#dusks-edge)

A [Judge](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-judge) priest who holds the senior civic office. Unexceptional in piety, exceptional in administration. He has dispatched three investigations into the [empty villages](../margins/phenomena.md#the-empty-villages). He has received no returns.

He is preparing to request military assistance from Ostmere — an unprecedented step for a Free Cities ruler. He has not yet made the request. Once he does, many things change.

### Speaker Hallis Veyn
> **Location:** Verak's Mouth, Free Cities
> **Age:** 52
> **Role:** Speaker of the Council of [Verak's Mouth](../kingdoms/free-cities.md#veraks-mouth)

Competent, deeply corrupt, broadly liked. The man who runs the largest Free City and its complicated merchant politics. He is aware, in broad strokes, of all three Free Cities' problems. He has not yet realised they are connected.

## Aldric's people

### Lord Caen Drost
> **Location:** Drost's Keep, eastern Drost's Fief, Ostmere
> **Age:** 49
> **Role:** Lord of Drost's Fief; [Aldric's](aldric.md) former commander

Forty-nine years old. Married to Lady Remna. Three children: Arren (nineteen, training for military service), Felis (sixteen, tutored at home), and young Varrick (nine). A steady lord. Competent. Mildly decent. The kind of man who, in better circumstances, would have been a good king.

Drost considered Aldric a friend. He has written one letter to Aldric during the leave period. He has received no reply. He is beginning to suspect what has happened — that Aldric is not coming back — but he has not wanted to confront the suspicion. He has other concerns. His youngest, Varrick, has been ill in a way that worries the household physician. His wife's brother is in the middle of a financial scandal in Verak's Mouth. A rising lord in the neighbouring fief is making moves Drost does not like.

He will, eventually, ride out to look for Aldric. Not yet.

### Sergeant Penren Hall
> **Location:** Dren, Ostmere
> **Age:** 38
> **Role:** Aldric's former second-in-command; now acting captain of the company

A career sergeant, promoted to acting captain after Aldric's departure. Hall is competent and bitter — he did not want the promotion, and he did not want to be rid of Aldric. He has his own suspicion of what happened. He has not shared it.

### Corporal Vorren Haid
> **Location:** Dren, Ostmere
> **Age:** 29
> **Role:** One of Aldric's former corporals

Aldric's youngest officer, who Aldric had marked for future promotion. Haid looked up to Aldric more than the other corporals did. Aldric's disappearance has hit him particularly hard. He has not yet done anything about it, but he is the kind of young man who might.

### Enna of Harrow's Wife
> **Location:** Harrow's Wife village, central Ashfields, Ostmere
> **Age:** 58
> **Role:** [Aldric's](aldric.md) mother; still living

Aldric's mother. A widow. Still lives in the family cottage in the village of Harrow's Wife, roughly two weeks' walk from Aldric's current chapel. She is in reasonable health. She grew what the family could grow until recent years; she now lives partly on what the village shares with her, partly on a small military widow's pension for her husband Vorrell's late service.

**She does not know her son is dying.** He has not told her. He has not visited her in eight months. She has written. He has not replied. She has told her neighbours, carefully, that her son is busy with military duties. She does not believe this herself. She believes, privately, that Aldric is unwell. She fears, privately, that he is dying in the way his father did. She does not know where he is.

She is his unresolved.

## The Voiceless

### The Elder of the Blue House (formerly Ilmar Tress)
> **Location:** The Blue House, Vaun
> **Age:** 79
> **Role:** The longest-vowed member of the [Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md) in the Blue House

Under a lifelong vow of silence for fifty-one years. Was, before the vow, a bookseller. Has not spoken a word in public in living memory (for most current Voiceless members, his vow predates their entire lives).

Six weeks ago, he stopped sleeping.

He now sits in his small room. He writes. He puts his notes in a wooden box sealed with an old Blue House seal that only he has opened in living memory. He will not let anyone see the contents.

He has not yet spoken his Final Word. It would be a significant event if he did. The members of the Blue House are, increasingly, sleeping poorly themselves.

### First Silent Harra Vess
> **Location:** The Blue House, Vaun
> **Age:** 63
> **Role:** Leader of the Blue House listening

Harra has been silent for thirty-one years. She is the acting leader of the Blue House and is responsible for managing its affairs — including the Elder's unprecedented writing.

She is drafting a letter to the First Silents of the other listenings. She has not sent it. She does not know what to say. She has begun writing and destroying the letter, each day for the last week.

### Therren of the Long Stair
> **Location:** Hollfen
> **Age:** 45
> **Role:** A senior member of the Voiceless listening in [Hollfen](../kingdoms/free-cities.md#hollfen)

Under a Long Silence (seven-year vow), currently in his fourth year. Was, before the vow, an information-merchant in the grey trades. Took the vow after an incident he has not explained to anyone. He is the only Voiceless member who has, in living memory, been accepted from Hollfen's information-trade into a full vow.

Therren may know things the other Voiceless do not. He cannot currently speak. He will be able to, in three years. Something may happen before then.

## The Architect order

### High Archivist Morren Velt
> **Location:** Varrentholm, Ostmere
> **Age:** 71
> **Role:** Keeper of the deep archive of the [Architect order](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect)

The person who has read the most of what is in the deep archive. Has been keeper for eighteen years. Answers only to High Speaker Ormund Vale, and only barely to him.

Morren has been refusing requests for access lately. Including requests from other senior Architect clergy. He has his reasons. He has not explained them.

### Sister Calla Brenn
> **Location:** Varrentholm (recently posted to the March of Seven Rivers)
> **Age:** 34
> **Role:** Junior Architect scholar with unusual academic interests

A promising young scholar whose research has focused on pre-Collapse linguistic fragments — specifically, a particular script that appears in Ashfield ruins. Her work has caught the attention of senior Architects, not all of whom are comfortable with it.

Calla was recently reassigned to the [March of Seven Rivers](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-march-of-seven-rivers) in what may be a convenient posting or may be an exile. She is going. She is not yet sure which it is.

She is the kind of person who will, if she encounters something strange, recognise it.

## The Wound order

### Hessel of the Deep Well
> **Location:** Underdam, Veth
> **Age:** 42
> **Role:** A [Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound) priestess investigating the sealed mines

The Wound priestess who has begun lowering a plumb-line with a bell on it into an air-shaft connecting to the deep workings of the [Deep Vein](../kingdoms/veth.md#underdam). She does this every night at the same hour.

Hessel has not yet reported what, if anything, responds. She has made one important observation: *the bell has, on three nights out of the last seven, come back up with a small amount of fine grey dust on its clapper.* The dust has a faint smell she cannot place. She has collected it in a small pouch. She has not shown anyone.

### High Mother Erra Vross
> **Location:** Serrath, Veth
> **Age:** 68
> **Role:** The senior [Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound) priestess in Veth

Erra is the most politically powerful religious figure in Veth. She advises the Prince-Marshal. She has not told him what Hessel is doing at Underdam. She has, however, sent her own instructions to Hessel: *continue. Report to me directly. Tell no one else.*

Erra is beginning to think about war in different terms than the War Council is. Her concern is not whether Veth should attack Ostmere. Her concern is whether something else is happening that will make the question of war irrelevant.

## The old women who know

An informal category — the practitioners of the [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md) and the "women who remember" across rural Varek. Most are not named in written records. A few are known by name beyond their own villages.

### Berra Olven of Merrith's Keep
> **Location:** Merrith's Keep, Ashfields
> **Age:** 81
> **Role:** The village's Old Rites practitioner

The old woman of Merrith's Keep, where the milk bowl has been found empty every night for four months. Berra is the one who has been putting the bowl out. She is watching. She has not told anyone in her village what she is watching for.

She has been making notes in a small book she keeps in her cottage. The notes are in a form of shorthand she developed herself, based on her grandmother's markings. No one else can read them.

### Henna of the Broken Pine
> **Location:** A small village near the Vorrwood, Vorrlands
> **Age:** 66
> **Role:** An Old Rites practitioner near the affected region

The old woman of a village whose name Henna has asked people not to share widely. Her village is the one where the words spoken at the forest's edge are now being **answered in a voice that sounds like the speaker's own.**

Henna has stopped her village from speaking the words. She has not explained why beyond "it is answering differently." Her village has, for now, accepted this. She does not know how long the silence will hold.

### Mella Veth (no relation to the ruling Veth line)
> **Location:** A high valley in the Greyspine
> **Age:** 74
> **Role:** A practitioner associated with the [Undergods](../faiths/buried-faiths.md#the-undergods)

A Greyspine old woman who still talks to the Undergods of her valley. In most valleys, the Undergods have gone quiet. In Mella's, they still answer. Mella has noticed that **her valley's Undergods have, in the last two years, begun saying things she does not understand** — things that do not seem to be answers to her questions, but messages addressed to someone else.

She has considered walking out of her valley to find someone who might understand the messages. She is old. The walk would be hard. She has not yet decided.

## Others

### Heller Vresh
> **Location:** Missing — Old Ferris, Vorrwood
> **Age:** 31
> **Role:** A [Dusk's Edge](../kingdoms/free-cities.md#dusks-edge) militia officer; lost during the second village investigation

Heller is the Dusk's Edge militia officer who did not return from a perimeter check during the investigation of [Old Ferris](../margins/phenomena.md#old-ferris). He has a wife, **Ressa**, and two children, **Orrin** (seven) and **Vellen** (three).

Whatever happened to Heller Vresh happened in a way that left no trace. Ressa does not know whether she is a widow or a wife. She has not yet been compensated as either. She continues to set a plate for him at dinner.

She is one of the small, quiet tragedies that will not be recorded in any official history.

### The traveller from Hesket
> **Location:** Various (on the road)
> **Age:** Unknown (claims 39)
> **Role:** Unknown

A figure recently noticed by some observant travellers on the roads of Ostmere. Speaks well. Travels alone. Wears no apparent allegiance. Claims to be from the village of Hesket, which exists but where no one can confirm his residence. Appears genuinely interested in the state of the world, asks perceptive questions, and then does not share his own conclusions.

He may be a scholar. He may be a spy — for which kingdom, unclear. He may be nothing more than an ordinary traveller with unusual patience.

He may be worth watching. He may be worth avoiding. It is not yet clear which.

## The list grows

This is a partial list. More figures exist. More will become relevant.

A worldbuilding principle: **every village has someone in it who knows something.** Every city has three or four quiet people whose names are not in any official record but whose observations, if collected, would describe more of the truth of the world than any king's archive.

Most of those people will never be recorded here. They exist regardless.
