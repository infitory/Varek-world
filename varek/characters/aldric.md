# Aldric

> **Type:** Character (protagonist)
> **Status:** Canonical
> **Current location:** A ruined chapel in the eastern [Ashfields](../world/regions.md#the-ashfields)

## Basic

- **Name:** Aldric. The name, nothing else. He dropped his family name years ago. He does not correct people who ask after it; he simply does not provide it.
- **Age:** Mid-thirties. Looks slightly older, in the way that soldiers and sick men do.
- **Origin:** Born in a small village called **Harrow's Wife** in the central Ashfields, part of [Drost's Fief](../kingdoms/ostmere.md#drosts-fief). A Provider-faith village. A village that never particularly distinguished itself.
- **Profession (former):** Captain in the standing army of [Ostmere](../kingdoms/ostmere.md), serving under [Lord Caen Drost](../kingdoms/ostmere.md#drosts-fief).
- **Profession (current):** None.

## Appearance

- **Height:** Average for an Ostmere man — about five foot nine.
- **Build:** Lean. Once more muscular; he has lost weight in the last year due to the still-cough.
- **Hair:** Dark brown, going grey at the temples. Kept short in the military way, though he no longer trims it regularly.
- **Eyes:** Grey-green. The right one has a small scar through the eyebrow from a training accident at nineteen.
- **Face:** Lined. Sun and cold have both worked on it. He does not look kind. He does not look cruel. He looks *tired* in a specific way that cannot be fully disguised.
- **Hands:** Calloused, with old small scars. The left hand trembles in the mornings. He holds it still with the right when he notices someone watching.
- **Posture:** Still carries himself like a captain. Straight back, measured movements. This is becoming harder to maintain.
- **Voice:** Low, measured. Speaks little. When he does, he is clear. A slight cough catches at the end of longer sentences.

## Equipment

Worn with dignity. Maintained out of habit.

### Weapons
- **A longsword** — hand-and-a-half grip. Ostmere military pattern. Notched along the upper third of the blade from honest use. He has replaced the crossguard twice and the grip-wrap three times. He sharpens it every seven days, whether he has used it or not.
- **A long knife** — carried at the belt. Single-edged. For eating and for killing; most Ostmere soldiers' knives serve both functions, and the knife is washed between uses.
- **A small hand-axe** — for wood and, if necessary, fighting. Not his preferred weapon.

### Armour
- **A mail shirt** (chainmail) — Ostmere pattern, short-sleeved, hip-length. Well-maintained. Heavy. He wears it only when traveling or when he senses trouble; otherwise it hangs on a peg in the main hall.
- **A gambeson** — padded cloth, worn under the mail. Several times repaired; the patches have patches.
- **A captain's pauldron** on his left shoulder. Dark steel, with the insignia of Drost's company **scraped off**. He took the insignia off himself, with the point of his knife, the night before he left his lord's service. He has not put another insignia on.
- **A weathered surcoat** in deep green — the colour of Drost's fief, now faded to the colour of old moss. No heraldry remains visible.

### Other
- **A leather satchel** — containing a whetstone, an oilcloth, a tinder-and-flint, a small bronze mirror (for signal use, originally; he does not use it now), a small stoppered bottle of a dark liquid (distilled spirits, for wounds and for drinking), and a folded letter he has never opened.
- **A wool cloak** — heavy, dark, functional.
- **A pair of good boots** — his best piece of equipment. He understands the importance of boots.

## The illness

Aldric has the **still-cough** — a slow wasting disease of the lungs that is common enough in modern Varek that it has a folk name. No physician has formally diagnosed him. He has not looked for one.

### How he knows
- **The cough** — deep, wet, comes in waves. Worst in the morning. Usually absent during the day. Returns at night.
- **The tremor** — in his left hand. Worst in the morning; gone by midday. He has had it for roughly fourteen months. It is slowly getting worse.
- **The tightness** in his chest. Not pain exactly. A feeling that his lungs are working against something.
- **The weight loss.** He has lost approximately fifteen pounds in the last two years. His belt no longer sits correctly.
- **The fatigue.** Not the fatigue of hard work. A different kind. The kind that does not lift.
- **The pattern he saw in his father.** His father died of the same illness twelve years ago. Aldric watched it. He knows the shape of it. He knows, roughly, what is left.

### What he expects
Two to three years, if he is lucky. He is not, in his estimation, lucky. He has planned for two.

## Personality

### Quiet authority
The kind of man a room adjusts to without knowing why. He does not impose. He simply *is*, and people respond to it. This has followed him out of military service; even in villages where nobody knows him, innkeepers pour him his drink first.

### Measured
Does not waste words or movements. Has learned, over years of command, that efficiency of speech is a form of respect — both for others and for himself.

### Dry humour
Used rarely. When deployed, it lands hard. He has a particular way of saying something grim as if it were mildly funny, which makes people laugh and then feel uneasy about having laughed.

### Private
Does not share easily. Has friends from his military years — most of whom believe he is simply on extended leave — and does not correspond with them. The one letter he has carried for three months, unopened, is from [Lord Drost](../kingdoms/ostmere.md#drosts-fief).

### Observant
A soldier's observation. Notices doors, distances, possible approaches, who is armed, who is watching him. He does this automatically, without thinking. It is not fully separable from who he is.

### At peace with dying, not at peace with this death
He has made peace with *dying*. He has not made peace with dying *here, like this, for nothing*. He chose the chapel specifically to make the death small, private, and uncomplicated. He has not entirely convinced himself that this is a good choice. He continues to think it is, most of the time.

### Faith
None. He went through the motions of the [Six-Faced God](../faiths/six-faced-god.md) in his military years, mostly [The Provider](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-provider) and [The Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound) as circumstances required. He stopped believing somewhere around his third battle, when the men who prayed and the men who did not died in roughly the same ratios. He has not practised any faith since. He does not mock the devout. He simply has nothing to add.

He still, however, observes certain [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md) — salt at the threshold, a coin left if he passes a crossroads, a word spoken before slaughter — not out of belief, but out of habit, and out of a vague sense that it does no harm.

## Background

### Childhood
Born in Harrow's Wife to a family of subsistence farmers with a history of military service. His father **Vorrell** fought in the last years of the [Veth Wars](../history/veth-wars.md). His mother **Enna** ran the household. He had an older sister, **Marra**, who died at thirteen of a fever when Aldric was nine.

He grew up on his father's war stories. Not glorified ones — his father was clear about what the war had been. But the stories were the shape of the family. Military service was expected.

### Enlistment
Enlisted at sixteen, as was standard for men of his village. Assigned to the company of Lord Drost — then a minor lord, still building his reputation — after basic training.

### Early career
Served as a regular soldier for six years. Saw no major action — the border with [Veth](../kingdoms/veth.md) was quiet during his early service. He did see the kind of small, ugly work that a standing army does: one minor skirmish with Veth border raiders, two responses to bandit activity in the northern [Greyspine](../world/regions.md#the-greyspine), and one episode he does not discuss, in which the company was ordered to enforce a tax collection in a starving Ashfield village.

It was the tax collection that began his decline toward silence. It did not cause it. It began it.

### Promotion
Promoted to sergeant at twenty-two. Promoted to captain at twenty-seven — young for the rank, in recognition of a particular incident during a border skirmish at a place called **the Black Ford**, where his quick decision saved the greater part of his company. The incident also killed eleven of his men. He does not consider the promotion a fair trade.

### Captaincy
Served as captain for seven years. Commanded a company of roughly a hundred and sixty men. Under his command, the company saw: two border skirmishes, several patrol actions, one long winter posting at [Dren](../kingdoms/ostmere.md#dren) during a year when a bad harvest caused unrest, and one episode — the one that broke him, in a way he does not fully understand — during which the company was deployed to disperse a group of [Hollow King](../faiths/buried-faiths.md#the-hollow-king) worshippers found meeting in an abandoned mill.

The dispersal was supposed to be bloodless. It was not bloodless.

He does not talk about this.

### The decision to leave
Eighteen months ago, Aldric began to notice the tremor. Six months after that, the cough. He knew immediately what it was — his father's illness, at his father's age. He knew the trajectory.

He served for another ten months while his body slowly confirmed what he already knew. He did not tell anyone. He continued his duties. He fought, in a small way, at the last border incident of his service.

Three months ago, he requested leave from Lord Drost. Drost granted it without question; Aldric was owed considerable accumulated leave. Aldric left his post, gathered his few personal possessions from his quarters at Dren, and began walking. He told no one he was not coming back.

He walked for two weeks. He did not have a specific destination. He found the ruined chapel on the edge of Drost's lands — land that was technically under Drost's authority, but had not been surveyed in a generation. He cleared the main hall. He reinforced the trapdoor to the underground room. He settled in.

### Lord Drost
Aldric's former commander. A competent, decent lord. **Lord Caen Drost** is forty-nine, married, with three children. He has not yet noticed that Aldric is not returning from leave. Within a few weeks, he will realise. He will be hurt — he considered Aldric not only a reliable officer but a friend — but he will not pursue the matter. Drost is the kind of man who allows people to choose their own endings.

### The letter
Lord Drost wrote to Aldric two weeks after Aldric's departure. The letter was forwarded through the standard army postal channels, which delivered it (as standard) to Aldric's last known location. A traveller brought it the rest of the way to the chapel. Aldric has carried it in his satchel for nearly three months. He has not opened it.

He does not know what is in it. He suspects it is either an instruction to return, or a farewell.

He will open it eventually. Not yet.

## The chapel

### Location
On the edge of eastern Drost's Fief, approximately two days' walk south-east from Drost's keep. The land here was cleared for farming during the [Long Reconstruction](../history/long-reconstruction.md) and subsequently abandoned when the soil failed. The nearest occupied village is half a day's walk north. Aldric has visited it twice, for supplies. He was not asked his business.

### The building
A small Provider chapel, built perhaps two hundred and fifty years ago. Abandoned for at least fifty. Stone walls, partly collapsed. A slate roof that leaks in several places. A small attached outbuilding (a storeroom, originally) that is effectively destroyed.

### The layout
- **The main hall** — the old worship space. The ivy has been cleared from the interior. The broken pews are stacked along the northern wall. The altar remains at the eastern end; Aldric did not move it. It has no purpose, but he did not want the purpose of removing it. A hole in the eastern roof lets in the morning light. He has a small fire pit near the centre, a crude iron pot, his weapons stand, his satchel hook, a small bench he made from a broken pew.
- **The underground room** — accessed through a heavy trap door with iron hinges Aldric reinforced. Low ceiling (just over six feet). Dry stone walls. Cool. This is where he sleeps. Bedroll, oil lamp, a locked chest containing what little he has of value — a few silver marks, a small pouch of his mother's jewellery (which he inherited and has never sold), a bone-handled knife that was his father's, and a folded garment he does not discuss.
- **The rest of the chapel** — untouched. Collapsed doorframes, plant-eaten walls, rubble. He walks past these portions every day without looking.

### Supplies
He has enough dried food for perhaps six weeks. He has a small store of firewood. He has a bow he has not used yet for hunting but will need to use soon. The stream half a mile away provides water. The forest around provides game, if he can be bothered.

He can be bothered. He has not yet had to be bothered much. This will change.

### Daily life
He wakes before dawn. He watches the morning light move across the eastern wall. He eats sparingly. He walks — usually to the stream, sometimes further. He tends to his weapons, his equipment, his body. He does not read, though he can; he has no books here. He thinks. He waits.

He has not yet decided what he is waiting for. He tells himself he is waiting for death. He is probably waiting for something else.

## What Aldric does not know

- About the [Draining](../margins/the-draining.md) in any theoretical sense. He knows the land is tired; he does not know why.
- About the [empty villages in the Vorrwood](../margins/phenomena.md#the-empty-villages). These events are months old but have not reached his isolated position.
- About the [sealed mines of Underdam](../margins/phenomena.md#the-sealed-mines-of-underdam). Too far away; too quiet.
- About the [ship incidents](../margins/phenomena.md#the-ship-incidents). The Siltmere is a world away from his chapel.
- About the [Long Walker](../margins/phenomena.md#the-long-walker). It has been seen in the central Ashfields; he is in the eastern Ashfields. The paths do not yet cross.
- About the pattern of it all. He does not read scholarly texts. He does not talk to clergy. He does not have the information to construct the pattern, even if he were inclined.

## What Aldric will become

Unknown. He is not a hero. He will not be a hero. Heroes, in Varek, do not exist.

He is, however, a man with certain qualities that will matter:

- He is **observant** — in a world where almost no one is seeing the pattern, the capacity to notice things is rare.
- He is **at peace with dying** — which means he can act without the self-protective calculations that govern most people's decisions.
- He is **geographically placed** — his chapel is in the path of something.
- He is **unobligated** — he has left the structures that would, otherwise, make him a cog in someone else's plan.

He may not survive the story. He almost certainly will not.

What he does with what remains of his time is the question.
