# Story Premise

> **Type:** Story guide
> **Status:** Working document — will evolve as the story develops

## The opening situation

It is **the Seventh Blue Moon** by the [Siltmere calendar](../kingdoms/siltmere.md) — the **four hundred and twenty-first year After the Long Silence** by Six-Faced reckoning — the **eighty-third year of the Third Peace** by Veth reckoning. In common usage, it is simply **this year**.

Autumn is early. The mornings are cold. The trees of the eastern [Ashfields](../world/regions.md#the-ashfields) have started to turn.

## Where each thread sits

### Aldric
[Aldric](../characters/aldric.md) is alone in a ruined chapel on the edge of eastern [Drost's Fief](../kingdoms/ostmere.md#drosts-fief). He has been alone for three months. He intends to be alone for the two or three years his body has left to give him.

His left hand trembles in the mornings. His cough is worst when he first wakes. The sky through the hole in the eastern roof is the colour of old pewter.

**He will not be alone by nightfall.**

### The other threads
- **King Edvar III** of [Ostmere](../kingdoms/ostmere.md) is preparing for the autumn court. He is not aware that the court will be, in retrospect, one of his most important.
- **Princess Iselle** is reading. She is looking for something.
- **High Speaker Ormund Vale** is reading something else — material from the deep archive he had, until recently, avoided.
- **Prince-Marshal Serak Veth IV** is in Serrath, reviewing his finances, and a meeting of his War Council is scheduled in nine days.
- **Lady Merris Tavell** is in Vaun, managing a trade dispute. She has asked an agent to investigate the [Sallowmark](../margins/phenomena.md#the-ship-incidents) incident in Keth. The agent is en route.
- **Warden Ildeth Korr** of [Hollfen](../kingdoms/free-cities.md#hollfen) has not been seen in public for fourteen days.
- **Speaker Arven Del** of [Dusk's Edge](../kingdoms/free-cities.md#dusks-edge) is drafting a request to Ostmere for military assistance. He has not sent it.
- **The Elder of the Blue House** in Vaun — a [Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md) member silent for fifty-one years — has stopped sleeping. He is writing. He will not share what he writes.
- **In a sealed mine beneath Underdam**, a sound continues.
- **In the Vorrwood**, the quieted areas expand by another foot overnight.
- **The Long Walker** continues walking north-east, toward the [Hollow Lands](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-hollow-lands).

## The protagonist's role

Aldric is not a chosen one. He will not save Varek. He is, in all likelihood, going to die before the story's end — and even if he survives all its events, his illness will kill him within a few years either way.

**What Aldric has** is a specific, unusual position:

- He is **alone**, which means no institutional loyalties constrain him.
- He is **dying**, which means self-preservation does not govern his decisions the way it governs other people's.
- He is **observant**, which means he notices things.
- He is **competent** at the kinds of things his life has trained him for — violence, endurance, command, reading people.
- He is **geographically placed** in a part of Varek that things are beginning to move through.

The story asks what such a person does with the time he has left, in a world that is beginning to end.

## What happens first

Something interrupts his isolation. The specific form of the interruption is still to be decided — whether it arrives at his chapel or whether he encounters it while out. Whether it is a person, a creature, an event, a message. Whether it is immediate violence or slow dread.

Candidates:

1. **A person arrives at the chapel.** Someone seeking shelter, fleeing something, or specifically looking for him. A traveller. A militiaman. A priest. A stranger who should not know he is here.
2. **Aldric finds something.** On one of his walks, he encounters a sign of what is happening elsewhere — a reader-bird, a quieted patch of forest, a figure in the distance, a body, a message.
3. **An old connection reaches him.** A letter arrives, or a messenger — from Lord Drost, from his mother, from the army. Something compels him to act.
4. **The chapel itself becomes the site.** Something about the chapel — its location, its history, its meaning — draws attention. Perhaps it was built on something. Perhaps it was consecrated against something.

These are not mutually exclusive.

## The shape of the story (provisional)

A three-stage arc:

### Stage one: the interruption
Aldric's isolation breaks. He is drawn out of his planned death and into the beginning of events. He resists at first. He relents because something gives him a reason to. The reason is not grand. The reason is, probably, **personal** — an obligation he did not know he still carried, or a person he cannot refuse.

### Stage two: the widening
Aldric moves through parts of Varek. He encounters the phenomena. He meets some of the named figures. He begins, without trying to, to be one of the few people accumulating the full picture of what is happening.

He does not become a leader. He does not rally a movement. He is simply in the right places, seeing what is there, and — because he is who he is — understanding some of it.

### Stage three: the ending
An ending worthy of the world. Not a victory. Probably not a survival. Perhaps a refusal. Perhaps a witness.

In a world that is dying, the question is not "can it be saved" — it almost certainly cannot. The question is what a person does, and what a person *is*, at the ending.

## Tone

Low dark fantasy. Specifically:

- **Grounded.** The texture of daily life — cold, hunger, effort, small comforts — matters more than the cosmic stakes.
- **Ambiguous.** What is happening in Varek has no single explanation. Even by the story's end, there may be no single explanation.
- **Lonely.** The characters are isolated from each other even when they are physically together. Communication is hard. Understanding is rare.
- **Brief moments of beauty** amid the grey. A well-cooked meal. A sunrise. A fire. The weight of a well-made tool in the hand. These are not contrasts to the darkness — they are what makes the darkness matter.
- **Occasional humour.** Dry. Often gallows. Never cute.
- **Violence that is slow, costly, and rarely clean.** Fights take time. Wounds take longer to heal than is convenient. Death is ordinary and specific.
