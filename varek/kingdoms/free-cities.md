# The Free Cities of the Vorrlands

> **Type:** Confederation (not a unified kingdom)
> **Cities:** Verak's Mouth, Hollfen, Dusk's Edge
> **Binding treaty:** The Three-Signatures Accord
> **Combined population:** ~200,000 (census unreliable)
> **Status:** Canonical

Not a kingdom. A confederation of three city-states in the eastern forest territories of [Varek](../world/overview.md), bound by a treaty called the **Three-Signatures Accord** that is four hundred years old and has been violated by every signatory at least twice.

The cities do not share a ruler. They share:
- A common language.
- A common fear of external domination.
- A common willingness to betray each other over trade disputes.

## The Three-Signatures Accord

The founding treaty, signed roughly four hundred years ago during the [Long Reconstruction](../history/long-reconstruction.md). Its core provisions:

1. No city may make war on another without cause recognised by the third.
2. No city may sign an exclusive trade or military arrangement with an external power.
3. Each city guarantees the independence of the others in the face of external threat.

The accord has been invoked approximately two hundred and forty times. It has been violated — at least technically — in approximately one hundred and eighty of those invocations. It has nevertheless held, in the sense that no city has ever fully conquered or abolished another.

## Verak's Mouth

> **Population:** 40,000
> **Ruler:** Speaker of the Council (currently Hallis Veyn)

The largest of the Free Cities. Sits at the junction of three rivers — the **Vorr**, the **Wend**, and the **Little Brack** — which give the city its wealth and its name.

### Government

Dominated by **six merchant houses**, each of which controls a district and maintains private guards. The houses are:

- **House Veyn** — river trade, banking.
- **House Korrith** — timber, construction.
- **House Brance** — food and grain import.
- **House Hessal** — textiles, luxury goods.
- **House Drask** — weapons, armour, private security.
- **House Tallow** — a polite euphemism. House Tallow deals in information, passage, and services that are not openly described. The other five houses pretend not to know exactly what House Tallow does. House Tallow knows exactly what the other five are doing.

The official ruler is the **Speaker of the Council**, an elected position currently held by **Hallis Veyn**. Competent, deeply corrupt, broadly liked. The Speaker is elected every four years by the six house heads plus seven representatives of the artisan guilds.

### Districts

- **Houses Row** — the six merchant house districts, each with its own character.
- **The Stitch** — the central merchant quarter where the three rivers meet. Where business is done.
- **Low Brack** — the labouring districts. Crowded. Functional. Dangerous after dark in specific ways.
- **The Green Wards** — the wealthy residential districts, set back from the rivers.
- **The Quiet Quarter** — where the clergy of [The Quiet](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-quiet) keep their listening-houses and hospices. Oddly peaceful.

### Notable institutions
- **The Great Exchange** — the largest commercial market in Varek.
- **The Bridge Courts** — where inter-house legal disputes are adjudicated. Famously slow.
- **The Veyn Bank** — effectively Varek's largest financial institution, despite operating as a merchant house rather than a sovereign bank.

## Hollfen

> **Population:** 25,000
> **Ruler:** Warden Ildeth Korr

The darkest of the three cities — literally and otherwise. Built in a river gorge where daylight reaches the streets for only a few hours each day. Lamp-oil is the city's largest single expense.

### Government

Ruled by a hereditary **Warden**, currently **Warden Ildeth Korr**, a woman in her fifties. The Warden's position passes by designation rather than strict inheritance — the sitting Warden names a successor, who must be confirmed by the **Council of Shades**, a body of perhaps twenty senior figures whose identities are not fully public.

**Warden Korr's public appearances are rare.** Her policies are implemented by a council that speaks for her. She has not been seen in public for fourteen days as of the current date, which is not yet unusual for her but is beginning to be noticed.

### Economy

Hollfen's economy rests heavily on what are locally called **the grey trades**:

- **Information.** Hollfen's intelligence network extends into every other Varekian polity. Information is bought, sold, traded, and occasionally destroyed.
- **Assassination.** Officially illegal. Practically organised. The Hollfen guilds that handle contract killings have their own internal rules, their own pricing, and their own standards of workmanship.
- **Smuggling.** Goods the other kingdoms consider illegal — certain substances, certain books, certain items of [Old World](../history/old-world.md) origin — pass through Hollfen to buyers elsewhere.
- **Legal trade** — yes, Hollfen also trades in ordinary goods: river fish, timber, leather. This is the respectable half of the economy and exists partly as cover.

### Districts
- **The Gorge** — the central commercial district, where the grey trades are conducted.
- **The Pale** — the foreign quarter, where merchants from Ostmere, Veth, and the Siltmere maintain houses under Hollfen protection.
- **Black Stair** — the district of the Warden's household and administration. Restricted access.
- **The Silent** — where listening-houses of the [Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md) cluster. Their largest concentration in Varek.

### Reputation

Hollfen is feared. It is also, oddly, trusted in specific ways. A contract made in Hollfen — even an illegal one — is remarkably likely to be honoured, because Hollfen's entire economy depends on its reputation for delivering what it has been paid to deliver. Breaking a Hollfen contract, from either side, carries consequences that are reliable and severe.

## Dusk's Edge

> **Population:** 18,000
> **Ruler:** The High Chapter (a council of twelve)

The smallest and most openly pious of the three cities. The strongest presence of [The Architect](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect) faction of the Six-Faced God outside of Ostmere. Built on a plateau above the deep forest.

### Government

Ruled by the **High Chapter**, a council of twelve that mixes clerical and merchant authority:

- Four senior priests (one each from The Architect, The Provider, The Judge, and The Quiet — notably, no representative of [The Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound)).
- Four merchant-councillors elected by the trade guilds.
- Four **Land-holders**, representing the smaller settlements in Dusk's Edge's hinterland.

The Chapter elects a **First Voice** from among its members for a three-year term. The current First Voice is **Speaker Arven Del**, a Judge priest of unexceptional piety and exceptional administrative skill.

### The Vorrwood problem

Dusk's Edge is the city closest to the [Vorrwood](../world/regions.md#the-vorrlands) proper — the deep, old forest where the [recent disappearances](../margins/phenomena.md) have occurred.

Three villages in the Vorrwood, all within a month's journey of Dusk's Edge, were found empty over the last eight months:

- **Halt-by-the-Wash** — emptied in early spring. Thirty-four inhabitants. None found.
- **Old Ferris** — emptied in late summer. Fifty-one inhabitants. None found.
- **Crane's Hollow** — emptied three weeks ago. Twenty-two inhabitants. None found. **The village's bell was still ringing** when the investigating party arrived — no wind, no visible cause. It rang for an hour after they arrived, then stopped.

The High Chapter has dispatched three separate investigations into the Vorrwood. None have returned. A fourth is being organised, slowly, because volunteers are becoming difficult to find.

### Districts
- **The Upper Chapter** — the clergy and administrative district. Well-maintained stone buildings, old.
- **The Market Terrace** — the commercial heart.
- **The Timber Run** — where lumber is processed before shipping.
- **The Edge** — the outermost district, nearest the forest. Population shrinking.

## Culture (shared across the Free Cities)

- **Language:** Vorrish — a distinct dialect of Varekian, with additional vocabulary from pre-collapse forest-country languages. Mutually intelligible with Ostmere speech but noticeably different.
- **Religion:** [The Six-Faced God](../faiths/six-faced-god.md) present but less dominant than elsewhere. [The Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md) strongest here. [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md) practised openly and publicly, especially in the smaller settlements. Certain cults of [the Drowned Mothers](../faiths/buried-faiths.md#the-drowned-mothers) and local forest-undergods are openly tolerated.
- **Dress:** Layered, practical, often dyed in deep forest colours (greens, browns, darker). Merchant-class dress is notably more flamboyant than elsewhere in Varek.
- **Food:** River fish, forest game, wild mushrooms, preserved berries, strong dark beer, a wine made from a particular berry that grows only in the Vorrwood. The wine is called **vrask** and is an acquired taste.
- **Holidays:** The Tri-Signing (spring, celebrating the Three-Signatures Accord), the Long Night (midwinter), the Root Day (autumn, a half-pagan harvest festival), and a local festival called **the Counting** in each city, celebrating the particular founding myths of that city.

## The Free Cities' Problem

Three empty villages. Three failed investigations. No explanation. No visible perpetrator.

The High Chapter of Dusk's Edge is considering requesting military assistance from Ostmere, which would be the first such request in sixty years and would require the consent of the other two cities. Verak's Mouth has agreed in principle. Hollfen has not responded.

**Warden Ildeth Korr has not been seen in fourteen days.**

Something is wrong. The Free Cities, who spend most of their political energy suspecting each other, are slowly realising they may need each other for something that cannot be handled by any of them alone.
