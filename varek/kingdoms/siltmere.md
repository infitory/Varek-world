# The Siltmere Ports

> **Type:** Three independent port cities
> **Cities:** Vaun, Keth, Ord
> **Combined population:** ~100,000
> **Status:** Canonical

The southern coastal trade-states. Not a unified polity. Three independent port cities, each governed by its own **Harbour Council**, each wealthy, each corrupt, each deeply engaged with the others in ways that oscillate between cooperation and quiet sabotage.

## Cultural distinction

The Siltmere is culturally distinct from the rest of [Varek](../world/overview.md). Its population descends partly from seafaring migrants who arrived during the [Long Reconstruction](../history/long-reconstruction.md) and never fully integrated. The Siltmere:

- Keeps its own calendar (the **Tidal Reckoning**).
- Speaks a trade-creole alongside common Varekian.
- Practises a localised version of [the Six-Faced God](../faiths/six-faced-god.md) that gives disproportionate weight to [The Provider](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-provider) and [The Quiet](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-quiet) — the aspects most congenial to merchants and sailors.
- Has its own customs of greeting, contract, mourning, and marriage.

The Siltmere sees itself as older and wiser than the inland kingdoms. The inland kingdoms see the Siltmere as corrupt and foreign. Both views contain truth.

## Vaun

> **Population:** 50,000 (the largest city in Varek)
> **Ruler:** The Council of Nine, led by First Speaker Lady Merris Tavell

Built on a series of artificial islands connected by stone bridges. The islands are partly natural, partly the result of centuries of dredging and landfilling. The city extends onto the mainland as well, but the **Inner Isles** are its heart.

### Government

The **Council of Nine** is composed of the heads of the nine oldest trading families:

1. **House Tavell** — shipping, banking. Currently led by **Lady Merris Tavell**, First Speaker.
2. **House Arren** — grain and food import/export.
3. **House Vos** — luxury goods, textiles.
4. **House Delward** — military supplies, mercenary brokerage.
5. **House Kestral** — spice, dye, and rare materials.
6. **House Orb** — timber, construction materials.
7. **House Meir** — the oldest fishing fleet.
8. **House Hale** — shipbuilding, harbour administration.
9. **House Pell** — insurance, bonds, financial instruments.

The **First Speaker** is elected by the Nine from among their own number every five years.

**Lady Merris Tavell** — forty years old. Inherited the position from her assassinated father. The assassination has never been formally attributed. She suspects one of the other eight houses. She has not pursued the matter publicly, because doing so would require her to acknowledge the assassination, and Siltmere politics prefers certain things to remain informal.

### Districts

- **The Inner Isles** — the oldest and wealthiest districts. The nine house compounds, the main market, the councils, the Tidefane.
- **The Spill** — the mainland residential districts. Dense, mixed, increasingly wealthy.
- **The Outer Quays** — shipping, warehousing, the labouring districts.
- **The Fogmarket** — a floating market held on boats in the harbour at certain tides. Semi-legal, deeply beloved.

### Notable institutions

- **The Tidefane** — the healing well. An [Old Working](../world/magic-and-supernatural.md#the-old-workings). Controlled by House Tavell and House Pell jointly. Access is priced.
- **The Long Quay** — the main shipping dock. The busiest single structure in Varek.
- **The Blue House** — the central listening-house of [the Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md) in Vaun. Home to **the silent old man who has stopped sleeping**, whose presence and writings are not yet known outside the house itself.

## Keth

> **Population:** 30,000
> **Ruler:** The Harbour Council of Keth (five members)

Further west than Vaun, specialising in overland trade with the [Ashfields](../world/regions.md#the-ashfields). Keth's wealth is older and quieter than Vaun's, built over centuries of patient middleman work between the inland kingdoms and the sea trade.

### Government

The **Harbour Council** has five members, all drawn from the old landed families rather than the newer merchant houses. Keth is less volatile than Vaun and less corrupt than Ord — this is a matter of degree rather than category.

Current council:
- **Lord-Merchant Esselm Varr** (chair)
- **Lady-Merchant Brendina Hale**
- **Lord-Merchant Orren Ketch**
- **Lord-Merchant Vanis Mord**
- **Lady-Merchant Jarra Ofal**

Keth's council meetings are famously short and famously effective.

### Character

Keth is the **quietly prosperous** Siltmere city. It lacks Vaun's glamour and Ord's vice. Its wealth is in grain futures, cattle contracts, and the slow accumulation of land-rents across the south-western Ashfields.

Much of Ostmere's grain exports pass through Keth. Much of Ostmere's imports enter through Keth. This makes Keth strategically important in ways that its modest population does not suggest.

### Districts

- **The Old Harbour** — the original port. Stone-built, compact, orderly.
- **The New Harbour** — the expanded dock area, built in the last two centuries.
- **Landside** — the inland-facing commercial quarter.
- **The Chapter Walk** — the clergy district, unusually prominent for a Siltmere city.

## Ord

> **Population:** 20,000
> **Ruler:** The Harbour Council of Ord (seven members, three compromised)

The easternmost Siltmere port, closest to the [Vorrlands](free-cities.md). Ord's economy includes a significant trade in goods routed from [Hollfen](free-cities.md#hollfen), which the other Siltmere ports officially disapprove of and privately profit from indirectly.

### Government

The **Harbour Council** has seven members. **Three of them answer, quietly, to Warden Ildeth Korr of Hollfen.** This is known in Vaun and Keth. It is not publicly acknowledged.

Current council:
- **Lord-Merchant Halden Vorr** (chair) — not compromised.
- **Lady-Merchant Iselm Brenne** — not compromised.
- **Lord-Merchant Kess Revon** — not compromised.
- **Lord-Merchant Ferrel Stith** — compromised (Hollfen).
- **Lady-Merchant Trevil Vane** — compromised (Hollfen).
- **Lord-Merchant Markam Dole** — compromised (Hollfen).
- **Lady-Merchant Norelle Hask** — independent but ideologically aligned with Hollfen's economic interests.

This gives Hollfen an effective majority on the Ord Council on any issue where it cares to exert influence. It rarely does so openly. Ord functions, from the outside, as an independent port.

### Districts

- **The Docks** — the main shipping area.
- **Bone Market** — the grey-trade district, where questionable goods from Hollfen and elsewhere are redistributed. Technically legal. Largely not.
- **Stone Row** — the old noble families' quarter.
- **The Lees** — the labouring districts, which extend further than the city's official boundaries.

### The Siltmere Problem

Over the past year, **three trading ships have returned to Siltmere ports empty of crew**:

- **The Hessal's Virtue** — returned to Vaun six months ago. Cargo intact. Ship's log ends mid-voyage, mid-sentence, in an entry about "a colour the captain had not previously seen."
- **The Sallowmark** — returned to Keth four months ago. Cargo intact. Ship's log ends normally, but the next day's entry is written in a different hand and says only "they have not come back." The ship was crewed by eleven. The log was not continued.
- **The Dawn-Sister** — returned to Ord two months ago. Cargo intact. Ship's log ends with a week of normal entries, followed by three pages of carefully drawn geometric patterns, followed by blank pages.

**Each Council has suppressed news of its own incident.** None yet realise the other two have had identical ones. The pattern, when it is finally noticed — and it will be, soon — will become one of the first clear signals that something is wrong at a scale beyond any single kingdom.

## Culture (Siltmere-wide)

- **Language:** Siltmere creole — a blend of Varekian and the old sea-tongues. Fully understood across the three ports. Mostly understood in coastal Ostmere. Alien in Veth and the deep Vorrlands.
- **Religion:** [The Six-Faced God](../faiths/six-faced-god.md), weighted toward [The Provider](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-provider) and [The Quiet](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-quiet). [The Drowned Mothers](../faiths/buried-faiths.md#the-drowned-mothers) openly revered in private households. [The Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md) strong in Vaun.
- **Dress:** Layered, often brightly coloured, with particular attention to sash-work that indicates trade and family. Siltmere dress is the most distinctive regional fashion in Varek.
- **Food:** Fish of every kind, seaweed-based dishes, preserved lemons and peppers (the Siltmere is the only Varekian region where citrus can be grown), rice (imported, expensive, reserved for celebrations), bread made with fish-flour in leaner years.
- **Calendar:** The **Tidal Reckoning**, based on a cycle of nine lunar months rather than Varekian twelve. The current year is **the Seventh Blue Moon** by this calendar.
- **Holidays:** The Tide-Mother's Day (early spring), the Counting of Sails (midsummer, a trade festival), the Mourning of the Lost (late autumn, for sailors who did not return), and the **Short Night** (winter solstice).
