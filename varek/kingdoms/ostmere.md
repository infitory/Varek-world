# The Kingdom of Ostmere

> **Type:** Kingdom
> **Capital:** Varrentholm
> **Ruler:** King Edvar III of the House Varrent
> **Population:** ~400,000
> **Status:** Canonical

The largest and oldest of the current kingdoms of [Varek](../world/overview.md). Ostmere occupies the central [Ashfields](../world/regions.md#the-ashfields) and the western slopes of the [Greyspine](../world/regions.md#the-greyspine). Of its roughly four hundred thousand people, perhaps a tenth live in cities and the rest in villages or small freeholds.

## Government

### The Crown

**King Edvar III of the House Varrent.** Sixty-one years old. In reasonable health. Without a clear successor.

His only legitimate son died of fever at eleven. His daughter, **Princess Iselle Varrent**, is twenty-four and unmarried — a situation the court discusses in lowered voices. The succession is a problem that grows more urgent each year and which Edvar has shown no inclination to solve.

Edvar is not cruel, not corrupt, and not particularly capable. He maintains order through inertia. The bureaucracy functions without requiring him to do much.

### The Council of the Crown

Advises the king on matters of state. Composed of:

- **The Lord Chancellor** — currently Lord Ferris Morrow, an aged but competent administrator.
- **The Lord Marshal** — currently Lord Vannis Therrick, a career military man who served in the tail end of the [Veth Wars](../history/veth-wars.md).
- **The High Chamberlain** — manages the royal household.
- **The Crown Voice of the Six Faces** — a senior priest from whichever face currently holds primacy; rotates every seven years. Currently **High Speaker Ormund Vale** of [The Architect](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect).
- **The Keeper of Coin** — master of the treasury.
- **Three elected representatives** of the major lords.

### The Lords

Below the crown, Ostmere is governed by roughly forty **lords** of varying rank, each holding a fief of varying size. The lords collect taxes, administer justice in their territories, and maintain small private retinues. They are required to provide levies to the crown in wartime and to remit a fixed share of their tax income annually.

**The Crown's problem:** several lords, particularly in the southern Ashfields where the [Draining](../margins/the-draining.md) is worst, have been quietly reducing their tax remittances and hiring larger private retainers. The crown knows. The crown does not have the resources to intervene.

## Military

The **Standing Army of Ostmere** numbers roughly six thousand men, held in permanent half-readiness along the Veth border. Additional levies can be raised from the lords for perhaps eight thousand more, but slowly and at political cost.

Military culture in Ostmere is practical and unromantic. Captains like [Aldric](../characters/aldric.md) command companies of fifty to two hundred men. Advancement is by seniority and proven competence rather than noble birth, which distinguishes Ostmere from [Veth](veth.md).

## Cities and Towns

### Varrentholm

**The capital.** A city of perhaps thirty thousand, built in successive rings around the original royal keep.

- **The Inner City** — ancient stone and tight streets. The palace district, the senior clergy houses, the Royal Court, the guild halls. Patrolled by the King's Watch.
- **The Outer City** — newer, more sprawling, increasingly crowded with displaced farmers from the failing Ashfield margins. The outer wards are not well patrolled after dark.
- **The Beneath** — the catacombs and sewer system below Varrentholm, partly [Old World](../history/old-world.md) construction. Contains the [Ticking Chamber](../world/magic-and-supernatural.md#the-old-workings), which produces a rhythmic sound every nine seconds. The crown does not discuss it.

### Holt

**A market town and river port** at the head of the Ashfield plain. Population eight thousand. Ostmere's primary grain hub. The annual harvest fair here is the largest in the kingdom, though it has shrunk noticeably in the last decade.

### Dren

**A garrison town** in the northern Ashfields, forty miles from the Veth border. Population five thousand. Essentially a military installation with a civilian suburb.

**This is where Aldric was stationed for most of his career.** He knows the layout of Dren better than he knows the village he was born in.

### Brell

**A mining town** in the lower Greyspine foothills. Population three thousand. Iron, mostly. The Brell mines are considered safe — none of the sealed-mine incidents (see [Underdam](veth.md#underdam)) have happened here yet.

### Aster's Rest

**A pilgrimage town** around a minor relic of [The Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound). Population fifteen hundred, tripling during the pilgrimage season. Unexpectedly wealthy. Controlled in practice by the Wound clergy and in name by a local lord who understands his place.

### Drost's Fief

**A cluster of villages** around the keep of **Lord Caen Drost**, [Aldric's former commander](../characters/aldric.md). Agricultural, quiet, well-administered.

Aldric's chapel lies roughly two days' walk to the south-east, in land Drost nominally controls but has not surveyed in a generation.

### Eldmere

A smaller market town in the western Ashfields. Population three thousand. Known for its annual cattle fair, its stubborn brewery tradition, and a legal dispute with the neighbouring Duckhold Fief that has been ongoing for sixty-one years.

### Harrow's Reach

A fortified town at the edge of the [Hollow Lands](low-kingdoms.md#the-hollow-lands). Population twelve hundred. The last real settlement before the depopulated north-west. Its garrison is nominally loyal to the crown and practically self-directed. The townspeople do not go far from the walls after dark.

## Economy

### Exports
- Grain (diminishing each decade).
- Iron (from Brell and smaller Greyspine operations).
- Wool.
- Timber from the western forests.
- Pilgrim-revenue from Aster's Rest.

### Imports
- Fish and salt from the [Siltmere](siltmere.md).
- Fine goods and manufactured items from the [Free Cities](free-cities.md).
- Greyblood from [Underdam](veth.md#underdam), when relations with Veth allow.

### Currency
The **silver mark**, minted in Varrentholm. Stable value. Widely accepted across Varek, though the [Siltmere ports](siltmere.md) prefer their own coinage for large transactions.

## Culture

Ostmere is the default Varekian culture for outsiders: moderate, agricultural, moderately pious, militarily traditional, suspicious of cities, suspicious of novelty, suspicious of Veth.

- **Language:** Common Varekian, with a western inflection.
- **Religion:** [The Six-Faced God](../faiths/six-faced-god.md), with [The Provider](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-provider) dominant in rural areas and [The Architect](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect) dominant in the capital. [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md) practised privately in nearly every household.
- **Dress:** Practical, woollen, earth-toned. Nobility wear darker colours and more fitted cuts.
- **Food:** Bread, root vegetables, preserved meat, thin beer. The wealthy add river fish and spiced wines from the Siltmere.
- **Holidays:** The Harvest Binding (autumn), the Long Night (midwinter), the Opening (first thaw), the Mourning Walk (late summer, for the war dead).

## The slow failure

Ostmere's stability rests on three pillars: a functioning (if slow) bureaucracy, the moral authority of the [Six-Faced clergy](../faiths/six-faced-god.md) who align with the crown, and the standing army.

All three pillars are cracked. The bureaucracy is understaffed. The clergy are increasingly factional. The army has not fought a war in sixty years and is full of men whose purpose is unclear.

Nothing has fallen. Nothing is obviously about to fall. But observant people — and there are a few — see the shape of what is coming, and it is not recovery.
