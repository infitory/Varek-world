# The Low Kingdoms

> **Type:** Marginal polities
> **Status:** Canonical

A collective term for the small, marginal polities that exist in the gaps between the major powers of [Varek](../world/overview.md). None of them are truly independent; all pay tribute to one or more of the larger kingdoms. They are worth naming because history has a way of running through them.

---

## The March of Seven Rivers

> **Location:** Border territory between [Ostmere](ostmere.md) and the [Siltmere](siltmere.md)
> **Ruler:** Nominally Margrave Nella Thorn (age 12), effectively her regent uncle Lord Hessel Thorn
> **Population:** ~30,000

A marshy border territory, nominally Ostmere-vassal, practically self-governing. The Seven Rivers give the March its name — they drain through it toward the Siltmere delta.

### The succession problem

Margrave **Nella Thorn** inherited the position at age eight, after her parents died of a fever that also killed most of the senior household. Her uncle, **Lord Hessel Thorn**, serves as regent and is widely disliked. He is not obviously corrupt — merely mediocre, petty, and uninterested in his niece's welfare.

Nella is now twelve. In four years she will come of age and take her own seat. Lord Hessel is said to be considering options.

### Notable features
- **Thornhold** — the seat of the Margrave, population two thousand. A fortified town on the largest of the seven rivers.
- **The Long Causeway** — a raised road through the deepest marshland, partly [Old World](../history/old-world.md) construction.
- **The Drown-Fields** — an area of former farmland that was lost to rising marsh over the last century. Now partially submerged and avoided.

## Kell

> **Location:** Far north, at the edge of the Frostwall
> **Ruler:** The Stonekeepers Council (a hereditary body of five families)
> **Population:** ~4,000

A single fortified town and its hinterland. Technically independent. Practically forgotten. Trades furs for everything else.

Kell survives by providing the only reliable trading post for whatever still exists in the deep north. Trappers, hermits, and occasional parties from [Graveholm](veth.md#graveholm) in Veth pass through Kell with goods that do not have conventional names — furs from animals the southern kingdoms do not know, resins from trees that grow only at altitude, certain stones.

### Notable features
- **The Stone House** — the fortified central keep of Kell, older than the town. Its foundations are [Old World](../history/old-world.md).
- **The Bone Market** (no relation to the district in [Ord](siltmere.md#ord)) — a small irregular market where northern traders bring goods.

### The decline
Kell's population has shrunk by roughly a third in the last forty years. Northern trade has slowly dried up. What is left is subsistence and memory.

## The Broken Counties

> **Location:** Southern [Greyspine](../world/regions.md#the-greyspine) foothills
> **Rulers:** Four separate lordships, each with its own count
> **Combined population:** ~60,000

A band of four small lordships caught between Ostmere, the Siltmere, and the mountain edges of Veth. Nominally independent; realistically auctioning their loyalty to whichever side pays most in a given decade.

### The four counties

- **Delmar** — westernmost. The largest. Currently leaning toward Ostmere; has for thirty years.
- **Stell** — mountain-edge. Lumber and iron. Officially neutral; in practice sympathetic to Veth, because most of its trade runs north.
- **Harrend** — southern, agricultural, poor. The weakest of the four. Its current count, **Count Arlis Harrend**, is a drunkard and a debtor; his county is effectively run by [House Pell](siltmere.md#vaun) of Vaun through a web of creditor arrangements.
- **Broff** — eastern, forested. The most independent. Traditionally refuses all alliances on principle. The current Countess, **Countess Merriel Broff**, is sixty-three and has outlasted four political crises through sheer stubbornness.

### Strategic importance
The Broken Counties are the corridor between the Siltmere and the southern Greyspine. Control of the Counties affects trade routes. No major power has ever fully conquered them, because doing so would require occupying four separate territories with four separate loyalties, and the effort has never seemed worth the prize.

This situation may be changing. Count Harrend's debts are nearly called. Countess Broff is old. Stell's count is young and ambitious.

## The Hollow Lands

> **Location:** Deep north-west, beyond settled Ostmere
> **Ruler:** None (officially Ostmere; practically no authority)
> **Population:** Unknown — likely under 1,000, scattered

Territory in the deep north-west, officially part of [Ostmere](ostmere.md), practically depopulated. What villages existed there were abandoned during the [Draining's](../margins/the-draining.md) worst decades. **Nobody has formally surveyed the Hollow Lands in thirty years.**

### What is known

- There are still people living in the Hollow Lands. How many, and what kind of people, is not clear.
- Certain ruins there are older and better-preserved than the ruins elsewhere. [Old World](../history/old-world.md) structures that the [Reclamation](../history/long-reconstruction.md#the-reclamation) never reached.
- Animals live there. More than elsewhere in Varek. Not entirely the right animals.
- The **Long Walker** — a figure recently reported in the eastern Ashfields — has been reported walking consistently in the direction of the Hollow Lands.

### The garrison

[Harrow's Reach](ostmere.md#harrows-reach) is the last real settlement of Ostmere before the Hollow Lands begin. Its garrison is nominally loyal to the crown. In practice, it operates with enormous autonomy and does not volunteer information about what it sees.

Harrow's Reach maintains a small detachment — the **Watchmen of the Edge** — whose job is to observe the Hollow Lands and report. They report almost nothing. What they report, they report cryptically. Their casualty rate is low but not zero.

### The old village of Carrowden

A settlement abandoned forty-three years ago during the Draining. It lies roughly twelve miles into the Hollow Lands from the border of Harrow's Reach.

Carrowden is not entirely abandoned. Something lives there. Not many. Not nothing. Local tradition at Harrow's Reach holds that Carrowden should not be approached, and that travellers who have been near it should not be let back into the town at night until they have been looked at by someone who knows what to look for. Whether this is superstition or practical wisdom is not clear, and the people of Harrow's Reach do not test it.

## Other minor territories

### The Free Steading of Ivren
A tiny independent holding in the Greyspine — essentially one village and its surrounding forest. Population six hundred. Ruled by its own elected council. Protected by its utter strategic insignificance.

### The Jerrasel
A group of offshore islands west of the [Siltmere](siltmere.md), thinly populated by fishing families. Nominally under Vaun's jurisdiction, practically self-managing. Approximately three thousand people spread across twelve small islands.

### The Hermits' Line
Not a polity. A loose tradition of solitary dwellers along a particular stretch of the Greyspine ridge. Religious hermits, exiles, old soldiers, the mad. Their numbers vary. They acknowledge no authority. They occasionally pass messages between each other via routes no one else uses.

Aldric considered joining the Hermits' Line before choosing the chapel instead. He decided he did not want company of any kind, even the distant company of others who had also chosen solitude.
