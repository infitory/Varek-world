# The Principality of Veth

> **Type:** Kingdom (technically Principality)
> **Capital:** Serrath
> **Ruler:** Prince-Marshal Serak Veth IV
> **Population:** ~160,000
> **Status:** Canonical

The northern mountain kingdom, occupying the high [Greyspine](../world/regions.md#the-greyspine) and the harsh plateau beyond. Population concentrated in fortified towns along a few viable valleys.

Veth has been a principality rather than a kingdom for nine generations — a distinction the Veth themselves resent and the rest of [Varek](../world/overview.md) uses deliberately.

## Government

### The Prince-Marshal

**Prince-Marshal Serak Veth IV.** Forty-eight years old. The fourth of his line.

Serak is a competent but cornered ruler. He inherited a military tradition and a treasury that can't quite support it. He is intelligent enough to know his kingdom is failing, and proud enough to refuse to let it fail publicly. His current strategy — maintain the appearance of strength, squeeze the remaining wealth from the nobility, and hope for something to change — is not working, and he knows it is not working.

He has a wife, **Princess-Consort Valleya**, who is eight years his senior, widely respected, and the more politically capable of the two. They have three children: **Prince Ivar** (twenty-two, military commander in the north), **Princess Serra** (nineteen, expected to be married to an Ostmere lord as a peace gesture — the negotiation has stalled for three years), and **Prince Doren** (fourteen, in the Ironhall).

### The War Council

The primary governing body. Composed of:

- **The Prince-Marshal** himself.
- **The Iron Captain** — commander of the Veth standing army. Currently **Iron Captain Havel Korrin**, a veteran of the last skirmishes of the [Veth Wars](../history/veth-wars.md).
- **The Keeper of the Hall** — master of the Ironhall and the royal household.
- **The Stone Voice** — senior priest of [The Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound), the dominant face in Veth.
- **The Coin-Steward** — master of the treasury. Currently a woman named **Haela Drev**, widely considered the most terrifying person in Veth.
- **Four Lords of the Peaks** — rotating representatives of the major mountain holdings.

### The Veth Lords

Below the Prince-Marshal are roughly twenty **Lords of the Peaks**, each holding a mountain holding or fortified valley. Unlike Ostmere's lords, Veth's lords are expected to be militarily active — they train troops, lead patrols, and are held responsible for border incidents in their sectors. Veth lordship is a working position, not a sinecure.

## Military

The Veth standing army is the most professional military force in Varek. Roughly three thousand men, full-time, well-equipped, trained year-round. Additional levies can bring this to perhaps ten thousand, though the lords resent providing them.

### Military culture

Veth men are expected to serve ten years under arms, beginning at age sixteen. Those who complete ten years and survive are called **Ironmen** and receive a small pension and the right to speak at village councils. Those who serve longer and distinguish themselves may become **Hallsworn** — permanent soldiers of the Ironhall, the closest Veth has to a nobility-by-merit.

Women in Veth do not serve militarily but manage holdings in their husbands' absence. Veth women are, accordingly, more powerful in domestic and economic affairs than women in [Ostmere](ostmere.md).

## Cities of Veth

### Serrath

**The capital.** A fortified city of eighteen thousand carved into the side of a mountain. Stone, slate, iron. Cold ten months of the year.

- **The Ironhall** — the Prince-Marshal's seat. A keep that has not been successfully besieged in its four-hundred-year history. Its walls are twelve feet thick in places. The oldest portions may incorporate [Old World](../history/old-world.md) stonework.
- **The Stone Temple** — the central shrine of [The Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound) in Veth. A severe, beautiful structure of dark stone and narrow windows. The annual Festival of the Cut takes place here.
- **The Low District** — where the common people of Serrath live. Cramped, functional, cold. Heated by communal fire-pits in winter.

### Kolven

**A high-pass fortress town.** Population six thousand, garrisoned. Controls the primary route between Veth and Ostmere. Changed hands three times during the [Veth Wars](../history/veth-wars.md).

Kolven is tense in a way other Veth towns are not — its population is mixed (Ostmere civilians remained after the last treaty), and the garrison commanders rotate frequently because the position is considered stressful.

### Underdam

**A mining city.** Population four thousand. Iron, silver, and [Greyblood](../world/regions.md#greyblood) in smaller quantities than further south.

**Two of Underdam's mines were sealed last year.** The official reason given was "structural instability." Nobody in Underdam believes this. Nobody asks again.

The sealed mines are the **Deep Vein** (sealed in late winter) and the **Halvers' Reach** (sealed six months later). In both cases, workers reported sounds from below — slow, rhythmic, like breathing, but at a frequency far too low. A few workers reported the sounds becoming intelligible, as if almost-words. Those workers were quietly relocated. Two of them have since died of causes that were not investigated.

**The sounds have not stopped.** They are now, as of recent weeks, audible through the floor of certain surface buildings in the city. This has not been made public.

### Graveholm

A smaller town at the foot of the pass to the Hollow Lands. Population two thousand. Lumber and fur trade. The northernmost reliable settlement in Veth.

### Hestrom

A fortified village on the eastern slope, population nine hundred. Nearly abandoned sixty years ago during the last war, repopulated slowly. The village's founding story is a massacre.

## Economy

Veth's economy is **structurally broken**. The mountains produce less food than the population requires. Veth imports grain from Ostmere during good years and from the Siltmere during better years, paying for it with iron, silver, and increasingly with loans that it has increasingly little capacity to repay.

### Exports
- Iron (high quality).
- Silver (diminishing).
- Greyblood (controlled export; much is used domestically).
- Soldiers. Literally — Veth mercenaries are hired by all other kingdoms, and the remittances they send home are a non-trivial portion of the economy.

### Imports
- Grain (essential).
- Salt.
- Fine goods.
- Almost everything that isn't stone, metal, or wool.

### The Debt
Veth owes money. To Ostmere. To Vaun. To two of the merchant houses of Verak's Mouth. The total debt is not publicly known. Coin-Steward Haela Drev knows the exact figure. The Prince-Marshal knows approximately.

## Culture

Veth's culture is military, austere, and proud in a way that outsiders find exhausting.

- **Language:** Veth Varekian — a harder-edged dialect with particular vocabulary for stone, cold, and military matters.
- **Religion:** [The Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound) dominant. Other faces present but secondary. The Veth variant of the faith is severe — sermons emphasise endurance, sacrifice, and the nobility of suffering. The clergy of the other faces are treated with respect but not deference.
- **Dress:** Heavy wool, leather, iron fastenings. Dark colours. Practical cuts. Nobility wear the same basic cuts in slightly finer materials.
- **Food:** Root vegetables, preserved meat, hard bread, strong beer, a spirit called **graveward** distilled from fermented root mash that tastes like medicine and is drunk like water.
- **Holidays:** The Festival of the Cut (midwinter, Wound-centred), the Oathbinding (spring, for Ironmen coming of age), the Long Watch (late autumn, for the dead of the Veth Wars).

## The approaching crisis

Veth's treasury has been depleted for thirty years. Its military tradition is expensive. Its land produces less than it consumes. The Prince-Marshal maintains the appearance of strength through increasingly thin means.

Another war with Ostmere would bankrupt Veth within two years. A war is becoming increasingly likely precisely because the Prince-Marshal's advisors have run out of other ideas. The hawks on the War Council are arguing for a preemptive raid on the Hangfall Pass, justified as "defensive positioning." The doves are arguing for a marriage alliance with Ostmere through Princess Serra. The Prince-Marshal has not yet chosen.

And now there is a sound beneath Underdam that nobody can stop.
