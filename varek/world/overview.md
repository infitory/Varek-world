# The Shape of Varek

> **Type:** World overview
> **Status:** Canonical

## Geography

Varek is a continent of roughly the size a man could walk across in a hundred days, if he were young and the roads were clear. Neither is usually true.

It is bounded on four sides:

- **West — the Cold Sea.** Grey, rarely fished. Storms come in from the west in late autumn and last weeks.
- **South — the Siltmere Coast.** Where the rivers drain. A wide, flat, foggy delta of silted marshland. The Siltmere ports trade here with regions further along the southern coast.
- **East — the Bitter Reach.** A body of water separating Varek from whatever is east of it. Most Varekians have never met anyone from the eastern shore. The few who have don't speak of them fondly.
- **North — the Frostwall.** A mountain range so severe that no serious attempt has been made to cross it in living memory. The passes that existed in the Old World are said to be closed.

## The feel of the land

The land itself is varied but has a common quality: **it feels used**. Forests are thinner than the old songs describe. Rivers are more often muddy than clear. The soil in most places is workable but unremarkable.

There are places of genuine beauty — the Greyspine in autumn, the eastern lake country, the high meadows of the Veth foothills — but even these feel like survivors rather than glories. The world was richer once. Everyone knows this. Nobody knows why it isn't anymore.

## Climate

- **Winters** are long and personal. Snow reaches the lowlands for three to four months in most years.
- **Summers** are brief and distrusted. Harvests are smaller every decade.
- **Skies** rarely go fully blue. The default colour is a bruised yellow in the morning and the grey of unwashed wool by afternoon.
- **Storms** come from the west and the north. Eastern storms are rare and unlucky.

## The sun

The sun rises every morning. It is not dramatic about it. Folk sayings describe the sun as *reluctant* or *tired*. Whether this is poetic convention or literal observation is debated among those who have nothing better to do.

The sun has not visibly changed in living memory. But older records — those that survived the [First Collapse](../history/first-collapse.md) — describe a sun that was noticeably brighter, noticeably warmer, and rose more quickly. This may be myth. It is widely believed.

## Population and settlement

Estimated population of Varek, across all polities: **roughly one million**.

The largest concentration is in [Ostmere](../kingdoms/ostmere.md) and the [Siltmere ports](../kingdoms/siltmere.md). [Veth](../kingdoms/veth.md) is sparser and more scattered. The [Free Cities of the Vorrlands](../kingdoms/free-cities.md) are densely populated in their cities and nearly empty between them. Vast territories — the [Hollow Lands](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-hollow-lands), parts of the deep Greyspine, the northern reaches — are effectively unpopulated.

Perhaps one in ten Varekians lives in something that could be called a city. The rest are in villages, small freeholds, or travelling.
