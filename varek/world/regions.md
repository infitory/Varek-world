# Regions of Varek

> **Type:** Geography
> **Status:** Canonical

Varek is divided into several broad geographic regions that cross political boundaries. A region is not a kingdom — a region is a kind of land. The kingdoms sit *within* and *across* the regions.

---

## The Ashfields

> **Location:** Western third of Varek
> **Polities:** Mostly [Ostmere](../kingdoms/ostmere.md); small portions in the [Low Kingdoms](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md).

A vast flat lowland that was once the breadbasket of Varek. Now the topsoil has thinned to a pale grey crust that grows grain reluctantly and in small amounts. **Nothing dramatic happened here** — no war, no curse anyone can name. It just stopped being good land.

Most villages here still function, still pay taxes, still observe festivals, but with a kind of dim persistence, as if they've forgotten what they were celebrating.

> *"The Ashfields don't feel cursed. They feel tired."* — common traveller's note

### Notable features
- [Aldric's chapel](../characters/aldric.md) sits on the eastern edge of the Ashfields, where the land begins to climb toward the Greyspine.
- The abandoned village of **Eld's Wake** lies a half-day north of the chapel. Emptied during the worst decade of the Draining, forty years ago. Structures partially intact.
- The **Long Road** — an [Old Working](magic-and-supernatural.md#the-old-workings) — runs east-west through the northern Ashfields. It never needs repair. No one knows why.

## The Greyspine

> **Location:** Central Varek, running north-south
> **Polities:** Split between [Ostmere](../kingdoms/ostmere.md) (western slopes), [Veth](../kingdoms/veth.md) (northern ridge), and [the Broken Counties](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-broken-counties) (southern foothills).

A long ridge of mountains running north to south through the centre of Varek. Not dramatic peaks — wide, weathered, dark with old pine forest. The passes are reliable but cold nine months of the year.

### Inhabitants
- **Above the treeline:** bandits, hermits, and things that aren't quite either.
- **Below the treeline:** mining communities cling to the slopes, extracting iron and a dark grey stone called **Greyblood**.

### Greyblood
Greyblood is a dark, dense stone found in deep seams in the Greyspine. Prized for construction — it weathers well and takes a good polish. Also blamed, quietly, for a higher rate of madness in miners who work the deep seams for too long. The clergy of [The Architect](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect) consider Greyblood spiritually suspect but use it in construction anyway.

### Notable features
- The sealed mines of [Underdam](../kingdoms/veth.md#underdam), where the sounds continue.
- The **Hangfall Pass**, the primary route between Ostmere and Veth. Contested during the [Veth Wars](../history/veth-wars.md).

## The Vorrlands

> **Location:** Eastern territories
> **Polities:** The [Free Cities](../kingdoms/free-cities.md) and the surrounding forest.

Wetter, darker, heavily forested. The trees here grow close and tall and block most light. Three independent city-states persist in the Vorrlands, each controlling a river junction. The forest between them is technically unclaimed and practically dangerous.

### The Vorrwood
The deep, old forest between the Free Cities. Creatures that are rarely seen elsewhere are sighted often here. The locals don't talk about them the way outsiders expect — not with fear, but with a kind of **grudging familiarity**, the way you talk about difficult neighbours.

### Notable features
- **The three empty villages** — [Halt-by-the-Wash](../margins/phenomena.md), **Old Ferris**, and **Crane's Hollow**. All found empty in the last year. No bodies. No signs of struggle.
- **The quieted zones** — areas around the empty villages where no animals live anymore. Expanding.

## The Siltmere

> **Location:** Southern coastline
> **Polities:** The [Siltmere ports](../kingdoms/siltmere.md) of Vaun, Keth, and Ord.

A wide, flat delta of silted rivers, marshland, and low fog that rarely fully clears. The sea here is grey and close. The three city-ports dominate: wealthy by Varekian standards, trade-dependent, and deeply corrupt in the comfortable way of places that have been corrupt for so long the corruption has become infrastructure.

### Cultural distinction
The Siltmere has its own politics, its own gods, and its own calendar. It tolerates the inland kingdoms the way a rich aunt tolerates poorer relatives. The population descends partly from seafaring migrants who arrived during the [Long Reconstruction](../history/long-reconstruction.md) and never fully integrated.

### Notable features
- **The Tidefane** — a well in the city of Vaun whose water heals minor wounds. An [Old Working](magic-and-supernatural.md#the-old-workings). Controlled by the Council of Nine.
- **The Bone Market** in Ord — where questionable goods from Hollfen are quietly redistributed.

## The Hollow Lands

> **Location:** Far north-west, beyond the edge of settled Ostmere
> **Polities:** Nominally Ostmere. Effectively nothing.

Territory in the deep north-west, officially part of Ostmere, practically depopulated. What villages existed there were abandoned during the Draining's worst decades. **Nobody has formally surveyed the Hollow Lands in thirty years.**

Things live there now. Not many. Not nothing.

See [Low Kingdoms: The Hollow Lands](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-hollow-lands) for more.

## The Frostwall

> **Location:** Northern border
> **Polities:** None.

The mountain range forming Varek's northern edge. Impassable in living memory. The [Old World](../history/old-world.md) is said to have had roads across the Frostwall. None of them exist anymore — whether they were destroyed, or simply covered, is not known.

Above the Frostwall is presumably land. What kind of land, who might live there, and whether the northern cold has always been this severe, are questions no one now can answer.
