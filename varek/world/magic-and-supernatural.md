# Magic and the Supernatural

> **Type:** World overview
> **Status:** Canonical

Magic, in Varek, is **rare, expensive, unreliable, and usually bad for you**. There are no wizards in towers. There are no glowing staves. What exists instead is a scattered set of practices — some inherited from the [Old World](../history/old-world.md), some improvised, some purely fraudulent — that occasionally produce real effects.

## Hedge-working

The most common form of magic. Village women (and occasionally men) who know certain herbs, certain words, certain small rituals.

### What it can do
- Ease a birth.
- Settle a fever.
- Find a lost animal.
- Mend a minor wound slightly faster than it would heal on its own.
- Occasionally, predict weather with better-than-chance accuracy.

### What it costs
The work costs the practitioner. Hedge-workers age faster than their neighbours, tend to infertility, often develop tremors or a cloudy eye by middle age. Most villages have one. Most villages pretend not to.

### Cultural status
Hedge-workers occupy an ambiguous position. They are needed but not trusted. They are tolerated by the [Six-Faced clergy](../faiths/six-faced-god.md) as long as they do not claim divine authority for their work. In Veth, they are illegal but practised anyway. In the Siltmere, they operate openly and sometimes wealthily.

## Scripture-binding

A clerical practice, officially unsanctioned, semi-tolerated. Certain passages from the texts of the [Six-Faced God](../faiths/six-faced-god.md), recited in the correct sequence with the correct materials, produce small effects.

### What it can do
- A ward against rot on stored grain.
- A blessing that slightly improves a crop.
- A binding that keeps a door from being opened by the wrong hand.
- A quieting that makes a child sleep through a fever.

### Success rate
Perhaps one in four attempts. The other three produce nothing, or worse, the opposite of the intended effect.

### Costs
Fatigue, sometimes memory loss, sometimes something worse. Clergy who practice scripture-binding heavily often develop what is called **the gap** — a progressive loss of the ability to remember specific people, starting with those most recently met.

### Which faces allow it
- [The Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound) clergy openly practise it and consider the cost spiritually meaningful.
- [The Architect](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect) clergy use it sparingly for structural protection.
- [The Provider](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-provider) clergy use it for harvest blessings but officially deny doing so.
- [The Judge](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-judge) clergy forbid it.
- [The Quiet](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-quiet) clergy use a specific variant for the dying.
- [The Unnamed](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-unnamed) — unknown. Nothing they do is publicly described.

## The Old Workings

Pre-collapse magic, found in fragments, understood by almost no one. When it works — which is infrequently and often disastrously — it works at scale.

### Known functioning Old Workings in Varek

- **The Long Road** in Ostmere — a stretch of road in the northern Ashfields that never needs repair. Runs roughly eighty miles east to west. Its surface is a material that is not quite stone and not quite metal. Walking on it feels slightly wrong in a way travellers cannot describe.
- **The Tidefane** in Vaun — a well whose water heals minor wounds. Controlled by the [Council of Nine](../kingdoms/siltmere.md#vaun). Access is restricted and priced accordingly.
- **The Weeping Stone** in the Vorrlands — a large standing stone that some claim predicts deaths within three days by weeping blood. The claim is disputed. The stone does, however, sometimes weep blood. What the blood is is not clear.
- **The Silent Arch** in Dusk's Edge — a freestanding stone archway in the city's oldest district. Sounds made beneath it do not carry outside it. Used for private conversations by the wealthy.
- **The Ticking Chamber** beneath Varrentholm — a sealed room in the royal palace that produces a rhythmic sound every nine seconds. Has done so for four hundred years. The crown does not discuss it.

### Attempting to replicate or extend

**Forbidden by every major power and attempted anyway.**

Scholars in all four kingdoms study the Old Workings in secret. Most attempts at replication produce nothing. Some produce partial effects. A small number — perhaps once per generation — produce catastrophic effects. The last recorded catastrophe was the **Hallsbridge Incident** sixty-three years ago, in which an attempt to reproduce a warding effect killed eleven scholars and rendered the building uninhabitable. The building still stands. It is still uninhabitable.

## Things called magic that are not

### Alchemy
Mostly real, mostly incremental. Practising alchemists can distil, refine, and transform materials in ways that are useful but not dramatic. The field is slowly advancing. Alchemists are respected in the Siltmere and the Free Cities and viewed with suspicion in Ostmere and Veth.

### Astrology
Entirely false. Widely believed. Court astrologers exist in every major capital. Their predictions are vague enough to be occasionally right.

### Cartomancy
Cards read for divination. False. Popular in the Siltmere.

### Bone-reading
**Occasionally accurate, for reasons nobody has explained.** Bone-readers cast small bones — usually the metacarpals of hares — and read patterns in how they fall. The practice is associated with the [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md). Its accuracy, when it is accurate, is usually about specific local matters rather than large events.

### Necromancy
Does not exist in any reliable form. Scattered accounts of the dead being contacted or reanimated appear in legend and in [Hollow King](../faiths/buried-faiths.md#the-hollow-king) heretical texts. Whether any of these accounts describe anything real is disputed.

## The Uncategorised

There is a final category, which nobody calls magic because calling it magic would be to admit it exists: **the things that are happening in the margins of Varek right now.**

- The empty villages in the Vorrwood.
- The sounds beneath Underdam.
- The dreams that spread like weather.
- The crewless ships returning to Siltmere ports.
- The increasing response rate of the [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md).

These are not magic. They are something else. They do not have a name yet.
