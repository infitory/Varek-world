# The Long Reconstruction

> **Type:** History (recovery period)
> **Period:** Roughly 300-80 years ago (approximately three centuries)
> **Status:** Canonical

Three centuries of slow, grinding recovery. The period during which modern Varek took its current shape.

## Overview

The Long Reconstruction is the period during which:

- Population slowly recovered from post-[Collapse](first-collapse.md) lows.
- The [Six-Faced God](../faiths/six-faced-god.md) was codified and spread.
- The modern kingdoms were formed.
- The [Old World](old-world.md) ruins were surveyed, decoded in part, and **deliberately suppressed**.
- The infrastructure of the current era was built — cities, roads, trade networks.

The Reconstruction was not a renaissance. It was a slow, stubborn rebuilding from very little. By its end, Varek had a functioning civilisation. It did not have a recovered one.

## The phases

The Reconstruction is conventionally divided into three phases:

### Early Reconstruction (approx. 300-220 years ago)

The period during which scattered communities began to reconnect and small regional centres emerged. The early Six-Faced faith began to form, initially in the south, spreading northward over several generations.

Notable developments:
- The first permanent roads between settled areas.
- The first formal trade agreements between proto-polities.
- The codification of the **Southern Liturgy** — the earliest form of what would become the Six-Faced faith, centred in what is now the [Siltmere](../kingdoms/siltmere.md).
- The re-establishment of basic literacy in religious communities.

### Middle Reconstruction (approx. 220-120 years ago)

The period of institutional consolidation. Kingdoms began to form. The Six-Faced faith spread and became politically powerful. The [Reclamation](#the-reclamation) occurred during this period.

Notable developments:
- **The founding of Old Merren**, which would become [Ostmere](../kingdoms/ostmere.md), approximately 210 years ago.
- **The establishment of the Principality of Veth**, approximately 180 years ago.
- **The signing of the Three-Signatures Accord** between the [Free Cities](../kingdoms/free-cities.md), approximately 400 years ago (predating the formal Reconstruction but relevant).
- **The formalisation of the Six-Faced God's High Council**, approximately 170 years ago.
- **The Reclamation.**

### Late Reconstruction (approx. 120-80 years ago)

The period of the first major wars between the consolidated kingdoms. This phase ends with the beginning of the [Veth Wars](veth-wars.md) eighty years ago, which are conventionally treated as a separate historical period.

Notable developments:
- The **First Ostmere-Veth War** (approx. 140 years ago) — a brief border conflict.
- The **Siltmere Consolidation** (approx. 130 years ago) — the three ports formally agreed on their current relationship.
- The **Plague of the Long Summer** (approx. 110 years ago) — a widespread disease that killed roughly a twentieth of Varek's population. The response to the Plague significantly strengthened the Six-Faced clergy, particularly [The Quiet](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-quiet) order.
- The beginning of the modern [standing army](../kingdoms/ostmere.md#military) tradition.

## The Reclamation

This is the darkest part of the Reconstruction, and the least publicly discussed.

### What it was

The **Reclamation** was a two-hundred-year programme, approximately 240 to 40 years ago, during which the clergy of the Six-Faced God systematically **sealed, buried, or destroyed** Old World ruins deemed "spiritually dangerous."

The programme was coordinated by the early High Council of Faces, with the [Architect order](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect) as its primary actor.

### What was targeted

Not all Old World ruins. The Reclamation was selective. Its targets included:

- Ruins where functioning [Old Workings](../world/magic-and-supernatural.md#the-old-workings) had been identified that the clergy could not control.
- Ruins where texts were found that described religious systems or cosmologies incompatible with the emerging Six-Faced faith.
- Ruins where "presences" — anomalous phenomena, unusual atmospheres, or reported entities — had been observed.
- A specific category of sites the Reclamation records describe only as **"the named"** — sites that were understood, by the senior Architect clergy, to contain or indicate something that had a name the clergy would not speak.

### How it was done

Methods varied:

- **Burial** — the most common. Ruins were covered with earth, often with entire villages relocated or built on top to prevent re-excavation. Much of the northern Ashfields and parts of Ostmere sit on buried Old World sites.
- **Flooding** — where geography permitted, rivers were diverted to inundate ruins. The Drown-Fields in the [March of Seven Rivers](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-march-of-seven-rivers) include at least one flooded Reclamation site.
- **Destruction** — structures were disassembled, their materials redistributed as ordinary stone. The more dangerous sites were destroyed thoroughly enough that their locations are no longer known.
- **Sealing** — walling off access. Some Old World buildings were sealed with heavy stonework and consecrated in ways that were, at the time, believed to contain whatever was inside.
- **Consecration** — building a Six-Faced chapel or shrine on top of the site, theologically "reclaiming" it.

### What was lost

A significant portion of the Old World's written and architectural legacy was lost in the Reclamation. Not to time. **To deliberate action.**

This is not widely taught. The standard histories of Varek mention the Reclamation briefly, treat it as a minor episode of religious consolidation, and do not discuss its scale or its intentional destruction of knowledge.

### What was preserved

The Architect order's **deep archive** preserves some of what was taken from Old World sites before their destruction. These materials are not shared beyond senior Architect clergy.

The deep archive is said (in the few oblique references that have leaked out) to include:

- Texts describing the Old World's religious system — the one the Six-Faced God replaced.
- Records of what was found at the "named" sites, including descriptions that senior Architect scribes have refused to transcribe into more accessible forms.
- The original texts used to construct the foundational documents of the Six-Faced faith, which reveal how that faith was **designed** rather than received.
- A small number of functioning Old World artifacts kept for institutional use.

The deep archive is located beneath **the Grand Seminary of the Architect** in [Varrentholm](../kingdoms/ostmere.md#varrentholm). Access is controlled by the High Speaker of the Architect and perhaps three or four others.

Ormund Vale, the current High Speaker, has read portions of the archive. He has begun, in recent months, to read more.

## The Plague of the Long Summer

Worth its own note. Approximately 110 years ago, a disease swept across Varek over the course of a summer that was notably longer and warmer than usual. The disease killed roughly five percent of the population.

Significant in three ways:

1. **It consolidated the Six-Faced clergy's political power**, because their response to the plague — particularly through [The Quiet](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-quiet) order's hospice work — was visible and effective in ways secular institutions could not match.
2. **It caused a shift in Varekian culture toward greater pessimism and religiosity**, effects that persist today.
3. **It may have been a minor Collapse-type event**. Some scholars believe the Plague of the Long Summer was not an isolated epidemic but part of the ongoing [Draining](../margins/the-draining.md) — a flare-up of whatever deeper process is reducing the vitality of the world.

## The founding of the modern kingdoms

### Ostmere (Old Merren)

Founded approximately 210 years ago through the unification of several smaller polities in the central [Ashfields](../world/regions.md#the-ashfields) under a single king. The unification was partly military, partly diplomatic, partly engineered by the early Six-Faced clergy who saw political unity as a theological goal.

The founding king was **Varrent the Builder**, ancestor of the current House Varrent. He ruled for thirty-one years and died in an accident that some later historians suspect may not have been accidental.

### Veth

Founded approximately 180 years ago when the military-aristocratic houses of the northern [Greyspine](../world/regions.md#the-greyspine) formally united under a single Prince-Marshal. The first Prince-Marshal was **Serak Veth the Elder**, whose descendants still rule.

Veth was founded as a **principality** rather than a kingdom because the founding houses wanted to emphasise that their authority was earned militarily rather than divinely sanctioned — a distinction that would become increasingly awkward as Veth grew more theologically aligned with [The Wound](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-wound).

### The Free Cities

The [Free Cities of the Vorrlands](../kingdoms/free-cities.md) predate the formal Reconstruction. The Three-Signatures Accord was signed approximately 400 years ago — very shortly after the Collapse, in a period when the three cities were the only major surviving urban centres in the eastern territories.

### The Siltmere Ports

The [Siltmere ports](../kingdoms/siltmere.md) formalised their current relationship during the Siltmere Consolidation, approximately 130 years ago. Before that, they had been a shifting set of five or six smaller port settlements. The consolidation reduced them to the current three and established the pattern of cooperative rivalry that still defines Siltmere politics.

## The Reconstruction's costs

The Long Reconstruction was, by most measures, a success. Varek emerged from three centuries of effort with functioning kingdoms, a unifying religion, stable borders, and a population that was slowly growing.

It was not, however, a **recovery**. The Old World was not restored. A great deal of what had been lost was not regained. And some of what had been lost was lost **twice** — first in the Collapse, and then again, deliberately, in the Reclamation.

The civilisation that emerged from the Reconstruction was built partly on suppression. The Six-Faced faith is, in part, an instrument of that suppression. The kingdoms, in part, inherit its terms.

Whether this was necessary is a question no one who was alive during the Reconstruction is still alive to answer. The Architect order believes it was. The [Hollow King](../faiths/buried-faiths.md#the-hollow-king) heresy believes it was a crime.

The current era will soon have to decide whether it agrees with either of them.
