# The Old World

> **Type:** History (ancient / pre-collapse)
> **Period:** Unknown start — ending approximately 400 years ago
> **Status:** Canonical (but fragmentary and contested)

The civilisation that preceded [Varek](../world/overview.md).

Its name is unknown, or rather, the names that survive — **Thessarid, Ohmir, the Lettered Dominion** — are contested and possibly refer to different polities, different periods, or different faces of the same thing. Scholars argue. Nobody is certain.

## What is known

The Old World was:

- **Larger** than Varek. Its territory extended beyond the current continent, possibly across what is now the [Frostwall](../world/regions.md#the-frostwall) and the [Bitter Reach](../world/overview.md#geography).
- **More sophisticated.** Its architecture, its medicine, its agriculture all exceeded what Varek now practises.
- **More literate.** Written records were widespread. Many classes of people — not just clergy and nobles — could read.
- **More connected.** Roads, waterways, and communication systems that Varek cannot match.
- **In possession of the Great Magic.**

Its ruins are **everywhere** — beneath fields, under cities, in the forests. Its script appears on stones, on pottery, on the walls of certain caves. It has been partially deciphered. The majority of it has not.

## The Great Magic

The Old World practised what the current era calls the **Great Magic** — a form of systematic, institutional magic that operated at civic scale, powered infrastructure, and was integrated into daily life.

Evidence of the Great Magic:

- **The Old Workings** — the few surviving fragments of Old World enchantments. See [Magic and the Supernatural](../world/magic-and-supernatural.md#the-old-workings).
- **Architectural evidence.** Ruins of Old World cities include structures whose function cannot be explained without magic — towers whose purpose is unclear, plazas laid out in patterns that had clear ritual significance, buildings constructed of materials that cannot be reproduced.
- **Textual evidence.** Partial translations of Old World texts describe the Great Magic operating at scales that modern Varek cannot conceive of — cities that healed their own damage, roads that maintained themselves, weather that could be adjusted for agriculture.
- **Relic evidence.** Small items of Old World origin occasionally surface. Some of them still work, after a fashion. Most do not.

**Whatever the Great Magic was, it no longer works.** Its residue — the Old Workings — functions in fragmentary ways. Most of it is inert.

## Scale and population

Estimates are speculative. Most scholars believe the Old World had a population at least ten times that of current Varek — that is, at least ten million people across the continent. Some estimates run much higher.

Cities of perhaps two hundred thousand or more existed. Under the current Varrentholm, traces of a city perhaps three times its size have been identified. Under certain Siltmere ports, submerged structures suggest the original coastline was further out and the cities were larger.

## Culture and society

Very little is known with certainty. What is surmised:

- The Old World's social structure was complex and stratified, but in ways different from modern Varek. The class system does not map neatly onto current nobility and commoner.
- Multiple languages were spoken, with a common administrative tongue. The script found in the ruins is a representation of this administrative language.
- Religion existed but took forms that modern scholars struggle to reconstruct. There is evidence of multiple deities, of institutions that resembled clergy, of temples. There is also evidence that the religious system was **undergoing change** in the Old World's final centuries.
- The arts flourished. Old World poetry, music, and visual art have partially survived in fragments. The music, when reconstructed by modern scholars, sounds strange — harmonies that the current Varekian ear finds unsettling.

## The final centuries

The period immediately preceding the [First Collapse](first-collapse.md) is the best-documented part of Old World history, because records from that era were the last to be made and the most likely to survive.

The Old World in its final centuries was **not at peace**. Records describe:

- Political tensions between major polities.
- Religious disputes that grew increasingly bitter.
- **A series of minor disasters** — plagues, failed harvests, unusual weather — that the Old World's institutions handled but that accumulated.
- A growing sense, in the literature of the period, that **something was wrong**. Not one specific thing. A diffuse wrongness. Poets wrote about it. Philosophers debated it. The common people, according to some texts, simply felt it.

The last few decades of the Old World produced an unusual amount of writing about **endings** — endings of ages, endings of covenants, endings of the world. This is sometimes dismissed as typical pre-collapse hysteria. It is also sometimes taken seriously.

## What the Architect order knows

The [Architect order](../faiths/six-faced-god.md#the-architect) of the Six-Faced God maintains the largest institutional archives in Varek. Within those archives, in the deep stacks that junior scribes are not permitted to access, are documents from the [Reclamation](long-reconstruction.md#the-reclamation) that describe things the Old World **did**, **discovered**, and possibly **caused**.

Some of these documents describe the burial of things found in Old World ruins — not artifacts but **presences**, **concepts**, **truths** — that the early Six-Faced clergy believed were too dangerous to be remembered.

The Architect order does not share this knowledge. Even its own junior members do not know it exists. Only the High Speaker of the Architect and perhaps three or four senior archivists have full access to the deep stacks.

## What the Hollow King's followers believe

The [Hollow King](../faiths/buried-faiths.md#the-hollow-king) heresy holds that the Old World was a civilisation that knew the true god — the Hollow King himself — and that the Collapse was caused by the god's displacement, enforced by the same theological movement that would later become the Six-Faced clergy.

The Six-Faced clergy deny this absolutely. They also hunt the heresy with an energy that some observers consider excessive.

## The unanswered question

**What was the Old World?**

A golden age that was lost? A civilisation that destroyed itself through hubris? A culture that reached too far and was stopped? A society that was, in the end, unable to sustain itself?

The Architect order believes one thing. The Hollow King heresy believes another. The [Voiceless](../faiths/voiceless.md) have their own theory, which they do not share. The ordinary people of Varek believe what their grandmothers believed, which is that the Old World was something lost, and the present is what remains.

All of them may be correct. All of them may be wrong. The Old World does not answer, and its ruins do not explain themselves.
