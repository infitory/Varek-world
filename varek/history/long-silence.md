# The Long Silence

> **Type:** History (post-collapse dark age)
> **Period:** Roughly 400-300 years ago (approximately one century)
> **Status:** Canonical (but very poorly documented)

The century following the [First Collapse](first-collapse.md). Almost no written records survive from this period. What is known is reconstructed from archaeology and folk memory.

## What is known

### Population

The surviving population of what had been the [Old World](old-world.md) was perhaps **one-tenth of pre-Collapse levels** — possibly less. Some regions were more depleted than others. The [Hollow Lands](../kingdoms/low-kingdoms.md#the-hollow-lands) and parts of what is now [Veth](../kingdoms/veth.md) may have been reduced by more than ninety percent.

The survivors were scattered in small, isolated communities. Most of these communities had lost contact with each other and lived, effectively, as if they were the last people alive.

### Regression

The Long Silence saw widespread regression across nearly every dimension of human civilisation:

- **Agriculture** — Old World agricultural techniques, which had relied partly on the Great Magic and partly on complex irrigation and soil-management systems, were beyond the capacity of surviving communities to maintain. Subsistence farming — the minimum possible — became the norm.
- **Architecture** — Old World stonework could not be replicated. New construction during the Silence was primitive: wood and earth, small structures, no multi-story buildings. Communities lived in the shadow of Old World ruins they could not maintain and did not know how to dismantle.
- **Literacy** — collapsed almost completely. Scribal traditions were broken. Only a handful of surviving Old World texts were preserved at all, and most of those were preserved by people who could no longer read them — kept as sacred objects rather than as knowledge.
- **Metallurgy** — regressed significantly. Old World metal-working techniques were lost. Communities reverted to simpler methods.
- **Medicine** — collapsed. Disease became a far greater killer than it had been in the Old World.
- **Language** — fragmented. Without the Old World's unifying administrative tongue, regional dialects diverged rapidly. By the end of the Silence, populations separated by only a few hundred miles could no longer easily communicate.

### The fragmentation

Before the Collapse, the Old World had been broadly unified — at least culturally and economically, if not always politically. By the end of the Long Silence, that unity was gone. What existed instead were:

- Isolated farming villages.
- A handful of small defensive settlements built in the shadows of Old World ruins.
- Itinerant groups — hunters, traders, religious survivors — who moved between settlements and carried news slowly.
- Communities that had retreated to deep forests, mountain valleys, or remote islands, sometimes not making contact with outsiders for decades.

**Much of Varek was, during the Silence, effectively empty of people.** The Hollow Lands were created during this period. Large portions of the [Ashfields](../world/regions.md#the-ashfields) were depopulated and would be slowly resettled over the following centuries. Portions of the [Vorrwood](../world/regions.md#the-vorrlands) have never been resettled.

## What did not survive

The Long Silence is named for the silence of its records. But it is also named — in some scholarly texts — for what simply **stopped existing** during it.

### Animals

Certain animals that populated the Old World did not survive into the post-Silence era. Bones found in Old World middens include creatures that are no longer seen anywhere in Varek. Whether they went extinct during the Collapse, during the Silence, or in the centuries immediately after, is not always clear.

Notable lost species:

- **The great elk** — a large horned mammal, considerably larger than the deer of modern Varek. Represented in Old World art. Not seen since.
- **The paleblue fish** — a freshwater fish said to run in enormous schools through the rivers of the Vorrlands. Vanished.
- **The call-bird** — a bird that mimicked human speech. Represented in Old World mosaics and poetry. Absent from all modern records.
- **The stone-deer** — a smaller deer with dark, heavy musculature and an unusual tolerance for cold. Lived in the Greyspine and the northern ranges. Gone.

### Plants

Several plants mentioned in Old World agricultural texts have no modern equivalent. They may have been specific cultivars that were lost when the communities that grew them died. Or they may have been species whose conditions for survival no longer exist.

### Languages

The Old World's administrative tongue survived only as the written script. Its spoken form is entirely lost. Several regional languages mentioned in surviving fragments are simply gone.

### Weather

This is the most disputed claim — that certain kinds of weather that existed in the Old World no longer occur in Varek.

Old World records describe:
- **Clear blue skies** lasting weeks, in all seasons. Modern Varek rarely has more than three days of clear sky in a row.
- **A particular kind of warm summer rain** that Old World poetry describes lovingly. No modern analogue.
- **Lightning storms with specific colours** that appear in Old World descriptions and not in modern records.

Whether the weather actually changed, or whether Old World writers were simply more lyrical, is debated. Most scholars believe **something** about Varek's climate shifted during or after the Collapse. The evidence is circumstantial but consistent.

## The survivors' theology

During the Silence, the surviving communities developed — or rather, retained and modified — religious practices that would become the basis of the modern faiths.

### The origins of the Six-Faced God

The [Six-Faced God](../faiths/six-faced-god.md) was not yet codified during the Silence. But the **elements** that would later be synthesised into its faith were forming:

- Small cults of what would become The Provider, focused on subsistence and survival.
- Funerary traditions that would evolve into the practices of The Judge and The Quiet.
- Fragmentary observances that would contribute to The Architect, The Wound, and the Unnamed.

These elements existed in isolation, in different communities, without unification. The unification would come during the [Long Reconstruction](long-reconstruction.md).

### The deepening of the Old Rites

The [Old Rites](../faiths/old-rites.md) likely predate the Old World itself. During the Long Silence, they **became more important**, not less. Communities cut off from organised religion, living in the shadow of ruins they did not understand, practised what their grandmothers had practised. The Rites were often the only continuous thread connecting the Silence communities to the pre-Collapse world.

This is why the Rites are so deeply woven into Varekian life today. They carried the surviving population through a century of darkness.

### The Hollow King's emergence

It is during the Long Silence — according to the heresy's own texts, which are not fully reliable — that the [Hollow King's](../faiths/buried-faiths.md#the-hollow-king) cult began to form. Small communities in what is now the southern Ashfields and the Broken Counties developed the worship of a displaced god, whom they understood to have been the true god of the Old World.

The worship was not unified during the Silence. It was practised differently in different communities. The unification — to the extent the Hollow King heresy has ever been unified — came later.

## What happened in the Silence that was not recorded

The absence of records is the defining feature of this period. It is also a reason for suspicion.

Some things must have happened during the Silence. Populations that had lost nearly everything had to make decisions — about leadership, about survival, about each other. Wars must have been fought between surviving communities. Migrations must have occurred. Cultural changes must have taken place.

**Almost none of this is documented.**

It is possible that the Silence simply produced no literate witnesses. It is also possible that things happened during the Silence which later generations **chose not to record** — events too terrible, too shameful, or too dangerous to commit to writing.

The Architect order's deep archives contain fragments that may date from this period. Those fragments are not shared.

## The end of the Silence

The Long Silence ended gradually, over perhaps a generation. The markers of its end include:

- The resumption of regular written records — at first sporadic, then increasing.
- The re-establishment of trade routes between separated communities.
- The emergence of the first **unifying institutions** — early proto-governments, the first forms of what would become the Six-Faced clergy.
- A population that had stabilised and was beginning, slowly, to grow again.

The period that followed — the [Long Reconstruction](long-reconstruction.md) — is much better documented.

## What the Silence left

Three things that the modern era inherits from the Long Silence:

1. **A depleted world.** The Draining arguably began during the Silence, or arguably continued from processes begun in the Collapse. Either way, the world that emerged from the Silence was not the world that had entered it.
2. **The Old Rites.** The continuous thread from the pre-Collapse era, carried through a hundred years of darkness by the people who had the least reason to preserve anything and who preserved them anyway.
3. **A deep, half-conscious cultural memory of something that cannot be remembered clearly.** Varekian culture is haunted. It does not know what it is haunted by. The ghosts are real. The source of the ghosts is not clear.

Varek is, in a real sense, a civilisation living in a house whose previous inhabitants died suddenly. It has furnished the rooms. It has painted over the stains. It walks past the closed doors without looking at them.

The doors are still there.
